### 🔹 **What is Cerluten?**

**Class:** Short peptide bioregulator derived from brain tissue, part of the Khavinson-style Russian organ-specific bioregulator line.
**Mechanism:**

- Stimulates neural growth, contributing to brain plasticity and supporting long-term cognitive resilience.
- Modulates neurotransmitter balance in ways associated with improved focus, learning capacity, and mental clarity.
- Reduces oxidative stress at the neuronal level, a mechanism relevant to lowering long-term risk of Alzheimer's, Parkinson's, and other neurodegenerative conditions.

**Primary focus:**

- Memory, concentration, and cognitive processing speed (particularly in aging populations), and general long-term brain health maintenance for those under high cognitive demand.

---

🔹 **Oral Capsule Dosage (primary route)**
**Range:** **20 mg per capsule**, typically **1–2 capsules taken 1–2 times daily** with food.
**Typical protocol:** Courses commonly run **10–30 days**, with periodic repeat cycles recommended for sustained brain-health support.
**Rationale:** The short peptide structure allows oral dosing to deliver a brain-tissue-specific signal without requiring injection.

---

🔹 **Cycle Length**
**Common cycle length:** 10–30 days per course.
**Off-cycle:** Periodic cycling, rather than continuous daily use indefinitely, is the more commonly reported pattern for long-term cognitive maintenance.

---

🔹 **Optimal Dosing Window**

- Taken **with food** to support consistent absorption.
- No specific circadian timing is emphasized in the source material beyond daily consistency.
