GHK-Cu

### **Understanding GHK-Cu**

GHK-Cu (Glycyl-L-Histidyl-L-Lysine-Copper) is a naturally occurring copper peptide found in human plasma.
**Subcutaneous (SubQ) Form**
This method involves injecting GHK-Cu just beneath the skin, where it gradually enters circulation. Unlike the topical form, which primarily targets the skin surface, SubQ administration allows for **deeper penetration and systemic effects throughout the body**. It is most often used in therapeutic or regenerative medicine settings rather than purely cosmetic ones.
**Benefits:**

- **Systemic Reach:** Once absorbed, GHK-Cu travels through the bloodstream, interacting with tissues throughout the body, not just at the injection site. This enables support for whole-body repair and anti-aging processes.
- **Tissue & Bone Repair:** SubQ delivery has been studied for its ability to stimulate collagen production, support bone regeneration, and accelerate recovery in tendons, ligaments, and muscles.
- **Wound Healing & Recovery:** Clinical research suggests improved healing times for injuries and surgical sites, making it useful beyond surface-level cosmetic purposes.
- **Anti-inflammatory Effects:** By modulating inflammatory pathways, GHK-Cu can help reduce chronic inflammation, which plays a role in aging, joint issues, and systemic disease.
- **Cellular Protection:** GHK-Cu influences gene expression related to antioxidant defense and DNA repair, giving broader anti-aging benefits compared to topical use.

**Best Uses:**
SubQ GHK-Cu is typically considered when the goal is **therapeutic regeneration**, for example, injury recovery, joint/tendon support, post-surgical healing, or systemic anti-aging. It’s less common in cosmetic routines since topical or microneedling delivery is easier and very effective for skin. **Always consult a medical professional before use.**
 
**Cosmetic/Topical Form**: The most common and widely researched form of GHK-Cu, available in serums, creams, and lotions.
**Benefits:**

- **Anti-Aging:** Stimulates collagen and elastin production, helping to firm loose skin, smooth fine lines, and reduce wrinkles. It also supports improved skin density and thickness.
- **Skin Repair:** Promotes wound healing and tissue regeneration, reducing the appearance of scars, sun damage, and hyperpigmentation.
- **Antioxidant & Anti-inflammatory:** Protects against free radical damage while soothing redness and irritation.

**Best Uses:** Ideal for daily skincare routines focused on anti-aging and repairing skin damage. Can be applied directly to the face and body.
 
**Essential Components of a GHK-Cu Serum**
A basic GHK-Cu serum formulation typically includes:

1. **GHK-Cu Peptide:** This is the star ingredient. It's typically sold as a cosmetic-grade powder or a liquid solution.
   - **Purity:** Look for high-purity GHK-Cu powder (often 99% or higher).
   - **Concentration:** The recommended usage rate is generally between 0.5% and 2%. For a 1% concentration, you would add 1 gram of GHK-Cu powder to 99 ml of your base.
   - **Solubility:** GHK-Cu is water-soluble and should not be used in oil-only formulations.
   - **pH:** The peptide is most stable at a pH between 5 and 7. Avoid using it with highly acidic ingredients like Alpha Hydroxy Acids (AHAs) or Vitamin C (ascorbic acid), as this can degrade the peptide.
2. **Base/Vehicle:** This is the liquid that will carry the active ingredients. A water-based solution is essential for GHK-Cu.
   - **Distilled Water:** Always use distilled water to prevent contaminants from interfering with your formulation.
   - **Hydrosols:** These are often used for their pleasant scent and added benefits (e.g., rose, lavender).
   - **Hyaluronic Acid (HA) Solution:** A pre-made hyaluronic acid solution is an excellent choice as it provides a hydrating, gel-like base that is easy to work with and enhances the serum's moisturizing properties.
3. **Humectants (Optional but Recommended):** These ingredients attract and hold moisture in the skin.
   - **Glycerin:** A classic and effective humectant.
   - **Hyaluronic Acid (HA):** As mentioned above, it can serve as both a base and a powerful humectant.
4. **Preservative:** This is a non-negotiable component for any water-based cosmetic product. Without a preservative, your serum is at risk of bacterial and fungal growth, which can be harmful to your skin.
   - **Common Choices:** Look for a broad-spectrum, water-soluble preservative that is approved for cosmetic use. Examples include Euxyl PE 9010 or Phenoxyethanol. Always follow the manufacturer's recommended usage rate.

### **The Do’s and Don’ts for your cosmetic GHK-Cu**

**Do’s (Best Practices with GHK-Cu)**

- **Do apply on clean skin** → Apply GHK-Cu serums/creams after cleansing and before heavier moisturizers or oils.
- **Do use consistently** → Benefits build over time with daily use.
- **Do pair with gentle hydrators** → Works well with hyaluronic acid, niacinamide, ceramides, and peptides.
- **Do store properly** → Keep in a cool, dark place; refrigeration is preferred and will extend stability.
- **Do patch test first** → Especially if you have sensitive skin or are combining with other actives.
- **Do pair with sunscreen (SPF 30+)** in the morning to protect skin while collagen remodeling happens. Make sure it does not contain zinc.

**Don’ts (Things to Avoid with GHK-Cu)**

- **Don’t combine with strong acids** → AHAs, BHAs, glycolic acid, and lactic acid can reduce peptide stability and effectiveness.
- **Don’t layer with Vitamin C (ascorbic acid)** → Direct vitamin C can destabilize copper peptides. If you want both, use vitamin C in the morning and GHK-Cu at night.
- **Don’t use with retinoids (Retinol, Tretinoin, Retinal)** → May cause irritation and reduce peptide performance. Alternate nights instead.
- **Don’t combine with benzoyl peroxide** → Can deactivate copper peptides.
- **Don’t over-layer actives** → Too many actives at once can irritate skin and prevent GHK-Cu from working optimally.
- **Don’t over-exfoliate before applying** → peptides need a healthy barrier to work well.

## **2% GHK-Cu Cosmetic Cream Recipe**

- **15 mL Neutrogena Hydro Boost**
- **0.3 g (300 mg) GHK-Cu pure lyophilized powder**
- **0.5 mL distilled or sterile water**

---

**Step-by-Step Instructions**

1. **Sanitize:** Thoroughly clean your workspace and all tools (mini spatula, mixing container, stirring rod) with 70% isopropyl alcohol. Let them air dry completely to prevent contamination.
2. **Scoop Out Product:** Use a clean, sanitized spatula to transfer a portion of the Neutrogena Hydro Boost into a separate, sterile mixing container. Leave the remainder in the original jar. This portion will be used for blending.
3. **Weigh the GHK-Cu:** Place your empty mixing container or a small sterile dish on your digital scale and tare it to zero. Carefully weigh out **0.3 g of GHK-Cu powder**. A precision scale (0.01 g accuracy) is required.
4. **Reconstitute the Peptide:** Add 0.5 mL of distilled or sterile water to the GHK-Cu powder. Swirl or gently stir until the peptide is fully dissolved into a clear blue solution. This ensures even dispersion and prevents clumping.
5. **Blend into Cream Base:** Slowly add the GHK-Cu solution to the small portion of Neutrogena Hydro Boost in your mixing container. Stir thoroughly until the mixture is uniform and slightly darker blue. This can take several minutes.
6. **Combine Back Into Jar:** Once evenly mixed, transfer this portion back into the main jar of Neutrogena Hydro Boost. Stir gently but thoroughly until the entire jar has a consistent color and smooth texture.
7. **Store and Use:** Close the lid securely. Store in a cool, dark place; **refrigeration is preferred** and extends stability. Apply as part of your skincare routine, ideally after cleansing and before heavier creams or oils.
