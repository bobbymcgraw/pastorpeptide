### 🔹 **What is Thymogen?**

**Class:** Synthetic dipeptide (Glu-Trp) modeled on thymic protein signaling — one of the more established Khavinson-group bioregulators.
**Mechanism:**

- A minimal two-amino-acid peptide built to reproduce the immune-regulating signal of natural thymus-derived peptides.
- Stimulates T-cell activity to strengthen immune defense mechanisms.
- Modulates cytokine production to reduce chronic inflammation without pushing the immune system into overactivation — the goal is balance, not just amplification.

**Primary focus:**

- Immune resilience in chronic illness, immunodeficiency, or age-related immune decline.
- Recovery support following infection, chemotherapy, radiation, or other immune-suppressing insults, and modulation in autoimmune contexts.

---

🔹 **Injectable (Intramuscular) Dosage**
**Range:**

- **50–100 mcg daily**, for **3–20 days** depending on severity of immune dysfunction.

**Rationale:** As a small synthetic dipeptide, Thymogen has documented injectable use at very low microgram doses reflecting its potency at the T-cell level.

---

🔹 **Intranasal Dosage**
**Range:**

- **One 25 mcg spray per nostril, twice daily**, for **3–10 days**.

**Rationale:** The intranasal route is used for preventive or therapeutic immune support as an alternative to injection, described in the same source material.

---

🔹 **Cycle Length**
**Common cycle length:** Short courses of 3–20 days depending on route and severity.
**Off-cycle:** Repeated in defined courses rather than continuous daily use; frequency tied to the underlying immune concern being addressed.

---

🔹 **Optimal Dosing Window**

- Daily dosing throughout a short defined course; intranasal dosing split morning/evening.
- Framed as a targeted immune-support course during periods of elevated need (illness, post-treatment recovery) rather than an everyday regimen.
