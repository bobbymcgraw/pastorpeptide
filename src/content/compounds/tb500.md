### 🔹 **What is TB-500?**

**Class**: Synthetic peptide fragment of thymosin beta-4 (amino acids 17–43).
**Mechanism**:

- Promotes **actin polymerization**, enhancing **cell migration**, **tissue remodeling**, and **wound healing**. TB4 maintains the actin cytoskeleton through G-actin sequestration, and this repair mechanism is itself controlled by the circadian clock—part of why TB4 (like DSIP) is best administered at night for optimal cell repair.
- Stimulates **angiogenesis** (formation of new blood vessels) and improves **tissue oxygenation**.
- Exhibits **anti-inflammatory** effects and reduces fibrosis. Specifically, TB4 works by stopping NF-kB (nuclear factor kappa beta) from transcribing pro-inflammatory cytokines, limiting inflammatory signaling coming from senescent cells. This is part of why TB4 is often paired with Thymosin Alpha-1 (TA1) as a senolytic/immune-modulating combination.
- Supports **muscle, tendon, ligament, and skin regeneration** by mobilizing progenitor cells to sites of injury. Increases cell motility/migration and decreases fibrous (scar) tissue growth in ligaments, tendons, and muscles, favoring functional tissue repair over scarring.

**Primary Focus**:

- **Tissue repair and regeneration** (muscle, tendon, ligaments, skin).
- **Wound healing** and recovery from injuries.
- **Anti-inflammatory support** in chronic injuries or tissue stress.
- **Potential cardiovascular and organ repair** (research context).

---

🔹 **Oral Dosage**

- **Not effective orally**; TB-500 is degraded in the digestive tract.
- Preferred administration is **subcutaneous or intramuscular injection** for systemic or localized effects.

---

🔹 **Injectable / Subcutaneous Dosage (most common)**

- **Range**: 2–5 mg per week, typically divided into **2–3 doses**.
- **Frequency**:
  - During **acute injury**: daily or every other day injections for 2–4 weeks.
  - **Maintenance / preventative use**: 2–5 mg weekly for 4–6 weeks.
- **Local vs systemic**: Can be injected near the injured site or systemically (subcutaneous away from the site) for general tissue repair.

Rationale: Low-dose repeated injections support tissue regeneration and reduce inflammation without overwhelming systemic effects.

Real-world compounded example: **3,000 mcg/ml vial (5 ml)**, dosed at **450 mcg SQ daily for 30 days**, for soft tissue repair and immune support.

---

🔹 **Intranasal Dosage (immune support focus)**

- **2,000 mcg/ml (15 ml nasal spray bottle)** — dosed at **1–2 sprays intranasally daily or as needed**.
- Framed specifically for **immune modulation** rather than tissue repair — the tissue/soft-tissue repair use case still relies on the injectable route above.

---

🔹 **Cycle Length**

- **Acute injury**: 2–4 weeks of daily or every-other-day dosing.
- **Recovery / preventative**: 4–6 weeks of weekly dosing.
- **Off-cycle**: 2–4 week breaks are often used before restarting if long-term use is desired.

---

🔹 **Optimal Dosing Window**

- No strict timing requirement; injections can be done **morning or evening**.
- Some users prefer **post-workout** to coincide with natural repair and recovery processes.
