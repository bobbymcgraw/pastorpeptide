### 🔹 **What is Mod-GRF (1-29)?**

**Class:** Modified growth hormone-releasing hormone (GHRH) analogue — this is the compound commonly referred to as **"CJC-1295 without DAC,"** distinct from **CJC-1295 with DAC**, which is covered separately on this site.
**Mechanism:**

- Binds GHRH receptors in the anterior pituitary, triggering release of stored growth hormone.
- Works synergistically with GH-releasing peptides like Ipamorelin to amplify natural GH pulses.
- Carries a short half-life by design, producing a quick GH spike rather than the sustained elevation seen with the DAC-modified version.

**Primary focus:**

- Lean muscle development and improved fat metabolism.
- Recovery and connective tissue support.
- Skin quality and collagen production via GH-driven pathways.

---

🔹 **The With-DAC vs. Without-DAC Distinction (important for dosing):** CJC-1295 **with** DAC uses a Drug Affinity Complex modification that extends its half-life to roughly 6–8 days, producing sustained GH elevation from a once-weekly injection. Mod-GRF (1-29) — **without** DAC — has a very short half-life instead, producing a quick, natural-feeling GH pulse rather than sustained elevation. That short half-life is exactly why this version requires multiple daily injections rather than one weekly shot: the entire point is to mimic the body's own pulsatile GH release pattern rather than flatten it into constant elevation.

---

🔹 **Oral Dosage:** Not viable; peptide is degraded in the GI tract. Subcutaneous injection is required.

---

🔹 **Injectable / Subcutaneous Dosage (only practical route)**
**Range:**

- **100–200 mcg per injection**, typically paired with a GH-releasing peptide like Ipamorelin for amplified effect.

**Frequency:**

- **1–3 times daily**, usually in a fasted state, to replicate multiple natural GH pulses across the day.

**Rationale:** The short half-life means a single daily dose doesn't sustain meaningful GH elevation — frequent dosing is what makes this version work as intended.

---

🔹 **Cycle Length**
**Common cycle length:** Similar to other GH secretagogues, often run in 8–12 week blocks.
**Off-cycle:** Because it mimics natural pulsatile release rather than sustaining constant elevation, it's generally considered to carry a lower desensitization risk than long-acting analogues, though breaks are still commonly used.

---

🔹 **Optimal Dosing Window**

- **Before sleep and/or post-workout** are the most commonly cited windows, mimicking the body's natural GH pulse timing.
- Fasted-state dosing is generally preferred to avoid blunting the GH response.

---

✅ **Educational Integration Example (injectable / subcutaneous)**

- **Example protocol:** 150 mcg Mod-GRF (1-29) paired with 200 mcg Ipamorelin, dosed 2x daily (morning fasted and pre-bedtime) for 8–10 weeks.
- The frequent dosing schedule is the main tradeoff against CJC-1295 with DAC's once-weekly convenience.

---

🔹 **Stacking Strategy (Educational / Complementary)**
Since Mod-GRF (1-29) is a GHRH analogue working through pulsatile GH release, complementary stacks mirror those used with CJC-1295 with DAC, adjusted for the more frequent dosing schedule:
🔸 **GH Pulse Amplification:**

- **Ipamorelin, GHRP-2, GHRP-6, Hexarelin:** GH secretagogues that synergize directly with Mod-GRF's pituitary-stimulating action.

🔸 **Recovery & Regenerative Support:**

- **BPC-157, TB-500:** Support connective tissue and general recovery alongside GH-driven repair.

🔸 **Metabolic & Longevity Support:**

- **NAD⁺ precursors (NMN, NR):** Support mitochondrial energy availability for GH-mediated anabolic processes.

**Rationale:** Because Mod-GRF (1-29) produces a natural pulsatile GH response rather than sustained elevation, it pairs especially well with GHRPs designed to amplify that same pulse rather than compounds meant to sustain baseline elevation.
