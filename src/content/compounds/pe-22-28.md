### 🔹 **What is PE-22-28?**

**Class:** Synthetic peptide fragment (derived from the neurotrophic protein **Activity-Dependent Neuroprotective Protein – ADNP**).
**Mechanism:**

- Acts as a **neuroprotective and neurotrophic fragment** of ADNP.
- Regulates **microtubule stability** via interactions with tau protein, supporting neuronal structure and axonal transport.
- Enhances **synaptic plasticity** and learning processes.
- Provides **anti-apoptotic and anti-inflammatory effects** in neuronal cells.
- Has shown efficacy in **reducing anxiety, depression, and cognitive decline** in preclinical models.

Primary focus:

- **Neuroprotection** in aging and neurodegenerative diseases.
- **Cognitive enhancement** through synaptic repair and neuroplasticity.
- **Mood regulation** with anxiolytic and antidepressant potential.
- Potential role in **autism spectrum disorder and neurodevelopmental conditions**.

---

🔹 **Oral Dosage**
Range:

- 200 µg – 1 mg daily (anecdotal and early research use).
- Sublingual delivery often preferred for absorption.

Notes:

- Like its parent ADNP fragment (NAP/CP201), designed for stability in non-injectable forms.

---

🔹 **Injectable / Subcutaneous Dosage**
Range:

- 100–500 µg SC daily or 2–3x/week (research use).
- Oral/sublingual is generally emphasized over injectable routes.

---

🔹 **Cycle Length**

- Neuroprotection and cognitive effects: **8–12+ weeks**.
- Wellness use: **ongoing low-dose administration** is considered reasonable in biohacking communities.

---

🔹 **Optimal Dosing Window**

- **Morning or midday** → enhances learning, focus, and mood during active hours.
- Avoid late-evening use to reduce the risk of sleep disruption.

⚠️ **Safety Notes**

- Human trial data is still very limited — most evidence comes from preclinical studies.
- Anecdotal reports suggest it is well tolerated at low doses.
- Overactivation of neuroplasticity pathways may cause anxiety, overstimulation, or disrupted sleep in sensitive users.
- Long-term safety in healthy populations is not yet established.
