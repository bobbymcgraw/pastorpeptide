### 🔹 **What is Sermorelin?**

Class: Synthetic **Growth Hormone-Releasing Hormone (GHRH) analog**; fragment of endogenous GHRH (29 amino acids).
MOL Changes | Biolab Shop | Research Data
Mechanism:

- Stimulates the **pituitary gland** to increase endogenous **GH secretion**.
- Supports **IGF-1 production**, promoting tissue repair, anabolic effects, and metabolic regulation.
- Mimics **natural GH pulsatility**, leading to more physiologic GH/IGF-1 rhythms compared to exogenous GH.
- Indirectly supports **fat metabolism, muscle growth, sleep quality, and recovery**.

Primary focus:

- **Anti-aging and wellness** via GH axis optimization.
- **Lean mass support** and **fat reduction**.
- **Tissue repair and recovery**, including bone and joint health.
- **Metabolic regulation** and energy enhancement.

---

🔹 **Oral Dosage**

- Poor oral bioavailability → not used orally.

---

🔹 **Injectable / Subcutaneous Dosage (most common)**
Range:

- **100–500 mcg SC daily**; commonly **200–300 mcg/day** for adults in anti-aging protocols.
- Best administered **daily** to mimic natural GH pulses.

Frequency:

- **Once daily**, preferably in the evening to align with physiological GH secretion peaks during sleep.

Rationale: SC injection ensures **reliable absorption** and physiologic mimicry of GH secretion.

---

🔹 **Cycle Length**

- Common cycles: **3–6 months**, followed by evaluation of IGF-1, body composition, and functional outcomes.
- Longer-term wellness: **ongoing low-dose use** may be employed, with periodic IGF-1 monitoring.

---

🔹 **Optimal Dosing Window**

- **Evening or bedtime** → aligns with natural GH peaks for sleep-mediated recovery and anabolic effects.
- May be split in some protocols for enhanced pulsatility, though once nightly is standard.

Paired with: Ipamorelin 100 mcg SC morning + resistance training + protein optimization.
Rationale: Mimics physiologic GH patterns to maximize recovery, fat loss, and lean mass gains.

⚠️ **Safety Notes**

- Side effects: mild injection site irritation, transient flushing, headache, or edema.
- IGF-1 levels should be monitored to avoid supraphysiologic elevation.
- Contraindicated in active cancer, uncontrolled diabetes, or hypersensitivity to peptides.
- Generally well tolerated; longer-term studies show good safety in adults using physiologic doses.
