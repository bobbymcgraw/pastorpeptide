### 🔹 **What is Crystagen?**

**Class:** Short peptide bioregulator derived from thymus tissue, part of the Khavinson-style Russian organ-specific bioregulator line.
**Mechanism:**

- Stimulates T-cell production and activity, reinforcing core immune defense mechanisms.
- Balances pro-inflammatory and anti-inflammatory cytokine signaling, helping tone down excessive immune activation.
- Supports overall immune homeostasis, relevant both to immune deficiency states and to overactive immune responses.

**Primary focus:**

- Immune resilience in immunocompromised individuals or those recovering from illness or stress-related immune suppression, and general modulation support in autoimmune or chronic inflammatory conditions.

---

🔹 **Oral Capsule Dosage (primary route)**
**Range:** **20 mg per capsule**, typically **1–2 capsules taken 1–2 times daily** with food.
**Typical protocol:** Courses commonly run **10–30 days**, and are frequently paired with other immune-supportive bioregulators like Thymogen.
**Rationale:** The short peptide structure allows for oral dosing intended to deliver a thymus-tissue-specific immune signal without requiring injection.

---

🔹 **Cycle Length**
**Common cycle length:** 10–30 days per course.
**Off-cycle:** Periodic cycling is the more typical approach for ongoing immune support, rather than continuous indefinite use.

🔹 **Stacking Strategy (Educational / Complementary)**
Since Crystagen is centered on **T-cell activity and cytokine balance**, complementary stacking tends to focus on the broader immune and inflammatory landscape:
🔸 **Immune-Focused Bioregulators:**

- **Thymogen, Vladonix, Bonomarlot:** Overlapping immune-modulating bioregulators frequently combined with Crystagen for layered immune support.

**Rationale:** Crystagen targets thymus-driven immune regulation specifically; stacking aims to **reinforce overall immune balance** without duplicating mechanisms unnecessarily.
