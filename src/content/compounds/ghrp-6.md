### 🔹 **What is GHRP-6?**

**Class:** Synthetic peptide—Growth Hormone Releasing Peptide (GHRP), hexapeptide class.
**Mechanism:** Binds to ghrelin (GHS-R1a) receptors in the pituitary to stimulate endogenous GH release. Enhances IGF-1 production and is known to significantly increase appetite via ghrelin pathway activation. Often used in combination with GHRH analogues (like CJC-1295) for synergistic GH release.
**Primary focus:** Increase GH and IGF-1 levels, support muscle growth, fat metabolism, recovery, tissue repair, and anti-aging effects. Also particularly useful for appetite stimulation in underweight or catabolic individuals.

---

🔹 **Oral Dosage (least common)**
**Range studied/used in research:** Oral administration is largely ineffective; peptide is degraded in the gastrointestinal tract.
**Typical protocol:** Not used orally; subcutaneous or sublingual administration is required for effective GH release.
**Notes:** Injectable or sublingual administration ensures consistent absorption and effect.

---

🔹 **Injectable / Sublingual Dosage (most common)**
**Range:** Commonly used at 100–300 µg per injection, 1–3 times daily depending on goals.
**Frequency:** Typically 2–3 times daily to replicate natural GH pulsatility. Pre-workout or pre-bedtime injections are common.
**Rationale:** Short half-life (~15–30 minutes) necessitates frequent dosing to maximize GH pulses and IGF-1 production.

---

🔹 **Cycle Length**
**Common cycle length:** 8–12 weeks is typical for muscle growth, recovery, or anti-aging protocols.
**Off-cycle:** 2–4 week breaks may be implemented to allow pituitary recovery and prevent GH receptor desensitization.
**Note:** Longer or repeated cycles may increase effectiveness but require monitoring IGF-1 levels.

---

🔹 **Optimal Dosing Window**
**Injectable / Sublingual:**

- Pre-workout or evening/bedtime dosing aligns with natural GH secretion.
- Daytime injections are less common unless splitting doses for multiple pulses.
