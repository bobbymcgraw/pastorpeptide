### 🔹 **What is GHRP-2?**

**Class:** Synthetic peptide—Growth Hormone Releasing Peptide (GHRP), part of the hexapeptide class.
**Mechanism:** Stimulates the pituitary gland to release growth hormone (GH) by binding to ghrelin (GHS-R1a) receptors. Enhances GH pulsatility, IGF-1 production, and can modestly increase appetite. Often paired with GHRH analogues (like CJC-1295) for synergistic GH release.
**Primary focus:** Increase GH and IGF-1 levels, promote muscle growth, fat metabolism, recovery, tissue repair, and anti-aging effects.

---

🔹 **Oral Dosage (least common)**
**Range studied/used in research:** Oral administration is largely ineffective; peptide is degraded in the GI tract.
**Typical protocol:** Oral forms are not used; subcutaneous injection is required for effective GH release.
**Notes:** Injectable or sublingual administration ensures proper absorption and efficacy.

---

🔹 **Injectable / Sublingual Dosage (most common)**
**Range:** Commonly used at 100–300 µg per injection, 1–3 times daily depending on goals (GH pulses, recovery, anti-aging).
**Frequency:** Typically 2–3 times daily for pulse replication. Often timed pre-workout or before sleep to coincide with natural GH peaks.
**Rationale:** Short half-life (~15–30 minutes) requires frequent dosing to maximize GH pulses and IGF-1 production.

---

🔹 **Cycle Length**
**Common cycle length:** 8–12 weeks is typical for muscle growth, recovery, or anti-aging protocols.
**Off-cycle:** 2–4 week breaks may be used to assess pituitary responsiveness and IGF-1 levels.
**Note:** Longer cycles may increase effectiveness but monitoring is recommended to avoid GH desensitization.

---

🔹 **Optimal Dosing Window**
**Injectable / Sublingual:**

- Pre-workout or early evening/bedtime dosing is common to leverage natural GH secretion peaks.
- Avoid random daytime injections unless splitting doses for multiple pulses.
