### 🔹 **What is Mazdutide?**

**Class:** Dual receptor agonist (GLP-1 and glucagon).
**Mechanism:**

- **GLP-1 receptor agonist:** Enhances insulin secretion, suppresses glucagon release, and slows gastric emptying, leading to reduced appetite and improved glycemic control.
- **Glucagon receptor agonist:** Increases energy expenditure and promotes fat oxidation.
  **Primary focus:**
- Weight loss and metabolic health improvement.
- Potential benefits in glycemic control and cardiovascular risk reduction.

---

🔹 **Oral Dosage:** Not applicable; administered via **subcutaneous injection**.

---

🔹 **Injectable / Subcutaneous Dosage (most common)**
**Range:**

- **Typical dosing:** Once-weekly injections, with doses ranging from 4 mg to 10 mg.
- **Administration:** Subcutaneous injection, typically in the abdominal area.

**Frequency:**

- Once weekly, as per clinical trial protocols.

**Rationale:**

- Once-weekly dosing provides sustained receptor activation, aligning with the pharmacokinetic profile of the drug.
- Subcutaneous administration ensures optimal absorption and efficacy.

---

🔹 **Cycle Length**
**Common cycle length:**

- **Induction phase:** 12 weeks.
- **Maintenance phase:** Extended duration based on individual response and clinical guidance.

**Off-cycle:**

- Not typically required; however, dosing adjustments may be made based on efficacy and tolerability.

---

🔹 **Optimal Dosing Window**

- **Timing:**
  - Administered once weekly, with no specific time of day required.
  - Consistency in administration day is recommended.
- **Notes:**
  - Monitor for gastrointestinal side effects, especially during the initial weeks of treatment.
  - Adjust dosing as necessary under medical supervision.
