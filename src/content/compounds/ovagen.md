### 🔹 **What is Ovagen?**

**Class:** Short peptide bioregulator derived from ovarian tissue (Khavinson-style oral bioregulator) — the female-reproductive counterpart to Testagen.
**Mechanism:**

- Signals to support the ovaries' own regulatory and hormone-producing capacity rather than supplying exogenous hormone directly.
- Associated with support for normal ovarian function, hormonal cycling, and reproductive tissue health as the ovaries age.
- Carries mild general metabolic and vitality support reported alongside its reproductive-axis activity, consistent with other organ-specific bioregulators in this family.

**Primary focus:**

- Female hormonal and reproductive health — ovarian function, cycle regularity, and reproductive tissue maintenance, particularly framed around age-related decline.
- General female vitality tied to a healthier hormonal baseline.

---

🔹 **Oral Dosage (primary route)**
**Range:**

- **100 mcg per capsule**, generally **1–2 capsules, 1–2 times daily**.

**Frequency:**

- Daily dosing for a minimum course of **20 days**.

**Rationale:** As an oral bioregulator, Ovagen is designed for GI-stable short-peptide signaling rather than systemic hormone replacement — the goal is nudging the ovaries' own regulatory machinery, not overriding it.

---

🔹 **Cycle Length**
**Common cycle length:** Minimum 20-day course; may be repeated or cycled based on how an individual's hormonal picture responds.
**Off-cycle:** Reassess hormone panels between courses before repeating; not intended as continuous daily use indefinitely.

---

🔹 **Optimal Dosing Window**

- Taken with or around meals per label convention for oral bioregulators.
- Best framed as a structured short course rather than an ongoing daily supplement.

---

✅ **Educational Integration Example (oral)**

- **Example protocol:** 100 mcg, 1 capsule twice daily, for 20 days, with hormone monitoring before and after the course.
- Sometimes discussed alongside general vitality- and metabolism-supportive bioregulators for a broader hormonal-health approach.

---

🔹 **Stacking Strategy (Educational / Complementary)**
Since Ovagen targets **female reproductive and hormonal function**, complementary stacks tend to focus on the broader hormonal and vitality picture:
🔸 **Reproductive & Hormonal Support:**

- **Testagen:** The male-tissue counterpart — not stacked together for the same person, but useful context for understanding how this organ-specific bioregulator pairing works.

🔸 **General Vitality & Recovery:**

- **Immune- and metabolism-supportive bioregulators:** Complement Ovagen's mild systemic effects without duplicating its reproductive-axis mechanism.

**Rationale:** Ovagen's role is narrow and organ-specific — the female reproductive axis — so stacking logic centers on supporting general resilience alongside it rather than layering other reproductive-axis compounds on top.
