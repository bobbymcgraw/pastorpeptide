### 🔹 **What is Kisspeptin-10?**

**Class:** Peptide hormone, short-chain fragment of the Kisspeptin family (RFamide-related peptides).
**Mechanism:**

- Binds to **GPR54 (KISS1R) receptors** in the hypothalamus.
- Stimulates **GnRH (gonadotropin-releasing hormone) release**, which in turn drives **LH and FSH secretion** from the pituitary.
- Indirectly supports endogenous **testosterone production in men** and **ovulation/fertility in women**.

**Primary focus:**

- Hormonal regulation of the reproductive axis.
- Potential fertility support and enhancement of endogenous testosterone production.
- Used in research on reproductive health, puberty, and metabolic-hormonal interactions.

---

🔹 **Oral Dosage:** Not effective; peptide degraded in the GI tract. Only injectable or subcutaneous administration is practical.

---

🔹 **Injectable / Subcutaneous Dosage (most common)**
**Range:**

- Typical dosing: 100 mcg – 200 mcg**per day** in research contexts, often divided into single or multiple injections.

**Frequency:**

- Once or twice daily subcutaneous injections are common in studies.

**Rationale:** Short peptide with rapid onset; subcutaneous injection ensures bioavailability and receptor engagement.
**Real-world example:** A compounding pharmacy protocol uses a 1000 mcg/ml vial (5ml), dosed at 100 mcg subcutaneously nightly for 30 days, indicated for increasing LH (luteinizing hormone).

---

🔹 **Cycle Length**
**Common cycle length:** 1–4 weeks in research protocols.
**Off-cycle:** Breaks may be used to prevent receptor desensitization and allow hypothalamic-pituitary-gonadal axis recovery.
**Note:** Limited human data; durations are largely extrapolated from animal studies and early clinical research.

---

🔹 **Optimal Dosing Window**

- Morning or early afternoon dosing aligns with natural hypothalamic activity.
- Avoid late evening dosing if stimulating LH/FSH near bedtime leads to sleep disturbances.

---

✅ **Educational Integration Example (injectable / subcutaneous)**

- **Example protocol:** 1 mg Kisspeptin-10 subcutaneous daily at 8 AM for 2 weeks.
- Can be paired with: Monitoring of LH/FSH levels to gauge pituitary response.
- Optionally combined with **HCG or HMG** in fertility or testosterone-support contexts.

---

🔹 **Stacking Strategy (Educational / Complementary)**
Since Kisspeptin-10 **stimulates the HPG axis**, complementary strategies focus on **enhancing endogenous hormone signaling and fertility support**:
🔸 **Male Fertility / Testosterone Support:**

- **HCG:** Maintains intratesticular testosterone while Kisspeptin stimulates pituitary LH.
- **HMG:** Adds FSH support for spermatogenesis.
- **Antioxidants (CoQ10, NAC, Vitamin C/E):** Support sperm quality and mitochondrial function.

🔸 **Female Fertility / Ovulation Support:**

- **HCG:** Used as ovulation trigger once follicles are mature.
- **FSH analogues or HMG:** Assist with follicular stimulation.
- **Antioxidants and mitochondrial support (CoQ10, NAD⁺ precursors):** Improve oocyte quality.

**Rationale:** Kisspeptin-10 acts upstream in the HPG axis; stacking focuses on downstream support and optimizing reproductive hormone signaling without duplicating Kisspeptin’s direct action.
