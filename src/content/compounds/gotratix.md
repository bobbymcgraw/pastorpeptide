### 🔹 **What is Gotratix?**

**Class:** Short peptide bioregulator (cytomax) derived from muscle tissue, part of the Khavinson-style Russian peptide bioregulator family.
**Mechanism:**

- Signals muscle fiber to accelerate its own repair and regrowth following training stress, injury, or surgical trauma.
- Builds resilience against physical stress, supporting strength and stamina over a course of use.
- Carries a mild anti-inflammatory action locally in muscle and connective tissue, which may ease soreness and stiffness after exertion.

**Primary focus:**

- Faster recovery of damaged or overworked muscle tissue, useful for athletes and anyone rehabbing after injury or surgery.
- Secondary support for joint mobility and general resistance to physical fatigue.

---

🔹 **Oral Capsule Dosage (primary route)**
**Range:** Commonly dosed at **21.5 mg per capsule**, with **1–2 capsules taken 1–2 times daily**, generally with food.
**Typical protocol:** A course of **1–2 months**, often timed around a training block, rehab period, or post-surgical recovery window.
**Rationale:** As with other short peptide bioregulators, Gotratix is built to survive oral administration and act locally on muscle tissue signaling rather than requiring injection.

---

🔹 **Cycle Length**
**Common cycle length:** 1–2 months, matched to the intensity of training or the demands of the recovery being supported.
**Off-cycle:** Cycled use rather than continuous daily use is the standard approach in this category — a break between courses is typical.

---

🔹 **Optimal Dosing Window**

- Taken **with food** to support absorption and minimize the mild digestive upset some users report at higher doses.
- Splitting the daily dose across morning and evening is a commonly reported approach rather than a single large dose.

---

✅ **Educational Integration Example (oral capsule)**

- **Example protocol:** 21.5 mg capsule, 1–2 capsules daily with meals, for 4–8 weeks, used around a heavy training cycle or a post-injury rehab window.
- Often discussed alongside other tissue-repair-oriented peptides as part of a broader recovery stack.

---

🔹 **Stacking Strategy (Educational / Complementary)**
Since Gotratix is focused on **muscle repair, endurance, and inflammation control**, complementary stacking tends to lean toward regeneration and joint support:
🔸 **Tissue Repair & Regeneration:**

- **BPC-157, TB-500:** General soft tissue repair peptides that complement Gotratix's muscle-specific regenerative signaling.

🔸 **Joint & Connective Tissue Bioregulators:**

- **Sigumir, Cartalax:** Cartilage and connective tissue-focused bioregulators that may pair well given Gotratix's own mention of joint mobility support.

**Rationale:** Gotratix targets muscle regeneration specifically; stacking aims to **extend recovery support into the surrounding connective tissue and joints** without duplicating the same mechanism.
