Tirzepatide

🔹 **What is Tirzepatide?**
**Class**: Dual GLP-1 and GIP receptor agonist
**Mechanism**:

- **GLP-1 receptor activation**: Enhances insulin secretion, inhibits glucagon release, delays gastric emptying, and reduces food intake.
- **GIP receptor activation**: Stimulates insulin secretion and promotes fat storage.

By activating these two receptors, tirzepatide aims to improve glycemic control, reduce body weight, and enhance metabolic health.
**Primary Focus**:

- **Type 2 diabetes**: Improves glycemic control.
- **Obesity management**: Significant weight loss observed in clinical trials.
- **Metabolic health**: Enhanced insulin sensitivity and reduced liver fat.

---

🔹 **Oral Dosage**

- **Not applicable**: Tirzepatide is administered via subcutaneous injection due to its peptide structure and poor oral bioavailability.

---

🔹 **Injectable / Subcutaneous Dosage**

- **Dosing**:
  - **2.5 mg** subcutaneously once weekly for 4 weeks.
  - Increase to **5 mg** subcutaneously once weekly for at least 4 weeks.
  - Further increase by **2.5 mg** every 4 weeks as needed, up to a maximum of **15 mg** per week.
- **Frequency**: Once weekly.
- **Rationale**: Weekly dosing aligns with the pharmacokinetics of tirzepatide, providing sustained receptor engagement.

---

🔹 **Cycle Length**

- **Duration**:
  - **Obesity management**: Clinical trials have typically lasted **72 weeks**.
  - **Type 2 diabetes**: Duration varies; consult specific clinical guidelines.
- **Evaluation**: Ongoing studies are assessing long-term efficacy and safety.

---

🔹 **Optimal Dosing Window**

- **Timing**: Administered at any time of day, with or without food.
- **Consistency**: Administer on the same day each week to maintain steady drug levels.

⚠️ **Safety Notes**

- **Gastrointestinal side effects**: Nausea, diarrhea, and vomiting are common, especially during dose escalation.
- **Hypoglycemia**: Monitor blood glucose levels, particularly in individuals with diabetes.
- **Contraindications**: Not recommended for individuals with a history of medullary thyroid carcinoma or multiple endocrine neoplasia type 2.
- **Availability**: Approved for use in several countries, including the United States and European Union.
