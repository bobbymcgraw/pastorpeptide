### 🔹 **What is BioBoost?**

**Class:** Injectable vitamin, mineral, and nutrient blend — not a peptide, but grouped here because it's used the same way many peptides are: as a regular add-on shot inside broader metabolic and wellness stacks. Similar in spirit to **Lipo-B** and **Lipo-C**, also covered on this site.
**Mechanism:**

- Delivers a concentrated mix of vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants directly into circulation, bypassing digestive breakdown.
- Supports cellular energy production, helping shuttle nutrients into the pathways that generate usable energy.
- Antioxidant components help offset oxidative stress, one of the underlying drivers of aging and chronic inflammation.
- Includes prebiotic/probiotic-supportive compounds aimed at gut flora balance, which feeds back into digestion and nutrient absorption.

**Primary focus:**

- General wellness support across immune function, digestion, and energy — a broad-spectrum nutrient repletion shot rather than a single-pathway compound.
- Commonly used as a supportive layer underneath a weight-management or performance protocol rather than as the primary driver of results.

---

🔹 **Route of Administration:** Injectable only (intramuscular or subcutaneous, depending on clinic protocol). BioBoost is formulated as a nutrient injection specifically because injectable delivery gives far more reliable absorption than an oral vitamin blend — there's no gut-breakdown or first-pass loss to work around.

---

🔹 **Injectable Dosage (standard route)**
**Range:**

- Typically dosed as a **1–2 mL injection**, once weekly.

**Frequency:**

- Weekly is standard; some protocols space it to every other week once initial repletion is established.

**Rationale:** Because BioBoost is a nutrient blend rather than a hormone or receptor-targeted compound, there's no real "loading" curve to manage — dosing is about maintaining steady nutrient status, not chasing a peak effect.

---

🔹 **Cycle Length**
**Common cycle length:** Typically used as an ongoing weekly adjunct for as long as the broader wellness or weight-management protocol runs, rather than in short discrete cycles.
**Off-cycle:** No mandated washout. Frequency is generally adjusted based on how the person feels and what else is in their stack, not a fixed on/off schedule.

---

✅ **Educational Integration Example (injectable)**

- **Example protocol:** 1–2 mL BioBoost weekly, layered alongside a GLP-1 or other metabolic-support peptide, with the goal of covering nutrient gaps that diet alone may not close during active fat loss.
- Frequently paired with a broader energy/recovery-focused stack rather than used on its own.

---

🔹 **Stacking Strategy (Educational / Complementary)**
Since BioBoost supports general nutrient status, energy metabolism, and gut/immune function rather than driving appetite suppression or hormonal signaling on its own, it complements compounds that handle those other jobs:
🔸 **Weight Management Synergy:**

- **Semaglutide, Tirzepatide, Cagrilintide:** Appetite and glucose regulation that BioBoost's nutrient and energy support can help sustain through a longer cut.

🔸 **Energy & Recovery Support:**

- **L-Carnitine, NAD⁺ precursors (NMN, NR):** Complementary support for mitochondrial energy production alongside BioBoost's broader nutrient base.

**Rationale:** BioBoost isn't trying to compete with hormone- or receptor-driven compounds — it fills in the nutrient and general-wellness gaps underneath them, which is why it shows up so often as a supporting player rather than the centerpiece of a protocol.
**Studied in Humans?** Yes — the individual nutrients (B-vitamins, minerals, antioxidants) that make up BioBoost each have a long clinical history on their own, though the specific fixed-dose blend itself hasn't been studied as a standalone clinical entity.
**FDA Approved?** No — not as a combination product. Several of the individual ingredients (like Vitamin B12) are FDA-approved on their own, but the BioBoost blend as formulated is not.
**Allowed for 503a Compounding Pharmacy?** Yes — commonly compounded.
