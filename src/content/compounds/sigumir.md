### 🔹 **What is Sigumir?**

**Class:** Short peptide bioregulator (cytomax) derived from bone and cartilage tissue, part of the Khavinson-style Russian peptide bioregulator family.
**Mechanism:**

- Stimulates chondrocyte activity, supporting cartilage cell regeneration and joint tissue repair.
- Reduces joint inflammation, relevant to osteoarthritis and other chronic joint conditions.
- Carries a mild immune-modulating effect that contributes to joint and tissue protection.

**Primary focus:**

- Joint health and cartilage repair, relevant to arthritis, joint injury, and degenerative joint disease.
- Secondary support for pain relief and improved joint flexibility.

---

🔹 **Oral Capsule Dosage (primary route)**
**Range:** Commonly dosed at **21.5 mg per capsule**, with **1–2 capsules taken 1–2 times daily**, generally with food.
**Typical protocol:** A standard **30-day course**, with cycled use depending on the severity of the joint condition being addressed.
**Rationale:** As a short peptide bioregulator, Sigumir is designed for oral absorption, acting locally on cartilage and joint tissue signaling.

---

🔹 **Cycle Length**
**Common cycle length:** 30 days per course.
**Off-cycle:** Cycled short courses with rest periods, rather than continuous daily use, is standard for this category.

---

🔹 **Optimal Dosing Window**

- Taken **with food** to support absorption and reduce the mild digestive discomfort some report during initial use.
- Temporary joint stiffness has been reported early in a course as cartilage regeneration gets underway — generally described as short-lived.

---

✅ **Educational Integration Example (oral capsule)**

- **Example protocol:** 21.5 mg capsule, 1–2 capsules daily with food, for 30 days, used as part of a broader joint-health and mobility routine.
- Often discussed alongside other connective-tissue and recovery-focused peptides.

---

🔹 **Stacking Strategy (Educational / Complementary)**
Since Sigumir is focused on **cartilage regeneration, joint inflammation reduction, and mild immune modulation**, complementary stacking tends to lean toward connective tissue repair and broader recovery support:
🔸 **Connective Tissue & Recovery Support:**

- **Cartalax, Gotratix:** Overlapping cartilage and muscle-tissue focus that may complement Sigumir's joint-repair mechanism.

🔸 **General Tissue Repair:**

- **BPC-157, TB-500:** Broader tissue-repair peptides often paired with joint-focused bioregulators for compounded recovery support.

**Rationale:** Sigumir targets cartilage and joint tissue specifically; stacking aims to **reinforce broader musculoskeletal recovery** without duplicating the same localized mechanism.
