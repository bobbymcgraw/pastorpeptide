### 🔹 **What is Fat Blaster?**

**Class:** Branded lipotropic injectable blend — not technically a peptide, but grouped here because it's used interchangeably with peptides in metabolic and weight-management protocols. It sits in the same product family as the site's **Lipo-B** and **Lipo-C** entries.
**Mechanism:**

- Typically formulated with **L-Carnitine**, **MIC** (Methionine, Inositol, Choline), a **B-vitamin complex**, and **NADH**.
- L-Carnitine supports transport of fatty acids into mitochondria for beta-oxidation and energy production.
- Methionine, inositol, and choline (the "MIC" trio) support fat mobilization, liver detoxification, and prevention of fat accumulation in the liver.
- B-vitamins support energy metabolism and nervous system function.
- NADH supports cellular energy production and mitochondrial function.

**Primary focus:**

- Supporting fat metabolism, liver function, and energy as an adjunct to a broader weight-loss or metabolic protocol.
- Not a primary weight-loss driver on its own — functions as a metabolic-support add-on, similar in role to Lipo-B and Lipo-C.

---

🔹 **Oral Dosage:** Not typically used; formulated specifically as an injectable blend for direct systemic delivery of its nutrient and amino-acid components.

---

🔹 **Injectable / Intramuscular Dosage (standard route)**
**Range:**

- Commonly dosed at **1–2 mL per injection**, 1–2 times weekly, consistent with dosing patterns for similar lipotropic blends like Lipo-B and Lipo-C.

**Frequency:**

- Weekly or twice-weekly, often administered alongside other weight-management visits or protocols.

**Rationale:** Injectable delivery bypasses digestive breakdown of the B-vitamin and lipotropic components, ensuring more reliable systemic availability than oral supplementation of the same ingredients.

---

🔹 **Cycle Length**
**Common cycle length:** Often used as an ongoing adjunct throughout an active weight-loss program rather than in short discrete cycles.
**Off-cycle:** No strict washout required; frequency is typically adjusted based on response and goals.

---

🔹 **Optimal Dosing Window**

- Morning or early-day dosing is commonly preferred, since the B-vitamin and NADH components can have a mild energizing effect.
- Avoid evening dosing in those sensitive to that energy boost, as it may affect sleep.

---

✅ **Educational Integration Example (injectable)**

- **Example protocol:** 1 mL Fat Blaster intramuscular weekly, used alongside a GLP-1 or other metabolic-support peptide protocol and paired with dietary and activity changes.
- Frequently used as a complementary add-on rather than a standalone weight-loss strategy — much like how Lipo-B and Lipo-C are typically positioned.

---

🔹 **Stacking Strategy (Educational / Complementary)**
Since Fat Blaster supports fat metabolism, liver function, and energy rather than acting as a hormone or appetite regulator, it pairs naturally with compounds that address those other levers:
🔸 **Weight Management Synergy:**

- **Semaglutide, Tirzepatide, Cagrilintide:** Appetite and glucose regulation that complements Fat Blaster's fat-mobilization and liver-support role.

🔸 **Same-Category Relatives:**

- **Lipo-B, Lipo-C:** Closely related lipotropic blends; formulations differ slightly by vendor, but the role in a stack is essentially the same.

🔸 **Energy & Metabolic Support:**

- **NAD⁺ precursors (NMN, NR), CoQ10:** Support cellular and mitochondrial energy production alongside Fat Blaster's B-vitamin and NADH contribution.

**Rationale:** Fat Blaster fills the metabolic-support and liver-health gap in a stack rather than driving the primary weight-loss mechanism, which is why it's most often paired with GLP-1-class compounds.
