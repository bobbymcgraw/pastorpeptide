### 🔹 **What is Bonomarlot?**

**Class:** Short peptide bioregulator (cytomax) derived from bone marrow tissue, part of the Khavinson-style Russian peptide bioregulator family.
**Mechanism:**

- Signals bone marrow to ramp up production of both red and white blood cells, supporting overall hematopoietic output.
- Shields hematopoietic stem cells from cumulative age-related wear and environmental stressors.
- Upregulates immune cell activity, with particular emphasis on T-cells and natural killer cells.

**Primary focus:**

- Blood cell production and bone marrow health, including recovery from anemia, low white cell counts, and marrow suppression following illness or surgery.
- Broader immune support and a general anti-aging effect on the hematopoietic system.

---

🔹 **Oral Capsule Dosage (primary route)**
**Range:** Commonly dosed at **20 mg per capsule**, with **1–2 capsules taken 2–3 times daily** alongside meals.
**Typical protocol:** A short course of **20 days**, extendable up to **3 months** depending on the goal (routine maintenance vs. recovery from a hematological insult).
**Rationale:** As a short peptide bioregulator, Bonomarlot is designed for oral absorption in a way longer proteins are not — the tissue-derived signaling peptides survive well enough intact to exert an organ-specific effect without needing injection.

---

🔹 **Cycle Length**
**Common cycle length:** 20 days to 3 months, scaled to the severity of the underlying concern (routine hematopoietic support vs. active recovery from marrow suppression).
**Off-cycle:** Bioregulator protocols are typically run as periodic short courses rather than continuously; allowing rest between cycles is standard practice in this category.

---

🔹 **Optimal Dosing Window**

- Taken **with meals** to support consistent absorption and reduce any GI irritation.
- Spacing doses across the day (2–3x) rather than a single large dose is the more commonly reported approach.

---

✅ **Educational Integration Example (oral capsule)**

- **Example protocol:** 20 mg capsule, 1–2 capsules with breakfast and dinner, for 20 days, used around periods of expected hematological stress (post-illness, post-surgical recovery, or intensive training blocks).
- Often discussed alongside other organ-specific bioregulators as part of a broader longevity-oriented stack.

Since Bonomarlot is focused on **hematopoietic and immune support**, complementary stacking tends to focus on immune modulation and general cellular regeneration:
