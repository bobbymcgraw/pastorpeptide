### 🔹 **What is GHK Base (No Cu)?**

**Class:** Naturally occurring tripeptide (glycyl-L-histidyl-L-lysine) — the copper-free version of GHK-Cu, which is covered separately on this site.
**Mechanism:**

- Stimulates collagen synthesis, supporting skin elasticity and texture.
- Activates gene expression pathways tied to DNA repair and cellular regeneration.
- Supports wound-healing signaling and tissue remodeling independent of copper-ion involvement.

**Primary focus:**

- Skin rejuvenation, texture, and anti-aging support.
- Wound healing and post-injury tissue recovery.
- General anti-inflammatory support for conditions like joint irritation.

---

🔹 **Note on GHK vs. GHK-Cu:** GHK-Cu (the copper-chelated form already covered elsewhere on this site) is the more extensively used and studied version, with copper playing a role in its enzymatic and angiogenic activity. GHK Base drops the copper component but retains meaningful regenerative signaling on its own — some formulators prefer it for users who want to avoid copper exposure or are sensitive to copper-containing topicals.

---

🔹 **Topical Dosage (most common)**
**Range:**

- Formulations typically range from **0.1%–1% concentration**, applied to skin.

**Frequency:**

- Once or twice daily on target areas.

**Rationale:** Topical delivery allows localized skin and wound benefit without systemic exposure, which is how most people encounter this peptide.

---

🔹 **Injectable / Subcutaneous Dosage**
**Range:**

- **1–5 mg per day**, used for broader tissue regeneration or anti-inflammatory goals rather than purely cosmetic ones.

**Frequency:**

- Daily dosing during an active protocol.

**Rationale:** Systemic delivery is used when the goal extends beyond localized skin care into general tissue repair support.

---

🔹 **Cycle Length**
**Common cycle length:** 4–8 weeks for topical skin protocols; injectable use is less standardized and typically shorter, goal-driven courses.
**Off-cycle:** Periodic breaks to assess skin/tissue response, particularly with topical long-term use.

---

🔹 **Optimal Dosing Window**

- **Topical:** Morning or evening on clean, hydrated skin.
- **Injectable:** No strong circadian rationale; consistency matters more than timing.

---

✅ **Educational Integration Example (topical)**

- **Example protocol:** 0.5% GHK Base serum applied nightly to the face for 8 weeks as part of a skin-rejuvenation routine.
- Often used in combination with other skin-support actives rather than GH-related stacking logic.

---

🔹 **Stacking Strategy (Educational / Complementary)**
Since GHK Base targets skin and tissue regeneration without copper's added enzymatic activity, complementary stacks lean toward skin and repair support:
🔸 **Skin & Tissue Repair:**

- **GHK-Cu:** The copper-bound version can be layered in when copper's added regenerative and angiogenic activity is desired.
- **BPC-157, TB-500:** Systemic tissue repair support that complements GHK Base's localized skin/wound action.

🔸 **Antioxidant & Collagen Support:**

- **Vitamin C and collagen peptides:** Support the same collagen-synthesis pathways GHK Base activates.

**Rationale:** GHK Base offers a gentler, copper-free entry point into GHK-family peptides; stacking focuses on reinforcing collagen and repair pathways rather than duplicating hormonal signaling.
