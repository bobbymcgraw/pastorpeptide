### 🔹 **What is Zinc Thymulin?**

**Class:** Zinc-dependent nonapeptide hormone naturally produced by the thymus gland (also called FTS — Facteur Thymique Sérique). Zinc isn't just an additive here — the peptide is biologically inactive without it bound in place, which is why every commercial formulation pairs the peptide with zinc.
**Mechanism:**

- In the body, thymulin plays a role in T-cell maturation and immune regulation, similar in spirit to other thymic peptides like Thymalin and Thymosin Alpha-1.
- The topical/dermatological interest comes from a different angle: research into hair follicles has found thymulin can help modulate the follicle's local immune environment, which is relevant in forms of hair loss (like alopecia areata and androgenetic alopecia) where immune activity around the follicle is thought to play a role.
- Applied topically, the goal is a localized effect on the scalp rather than a systemic immune one.

**Primary focus:**

- Hair loss support — the dominant use case in practice, applied directly to the scalp.
- Secondary interest in general thymic/immune support, though this isn't the typical reason people reach for the zinc-bound topical version specifically.

---

🔹 **Topical Dosage (primary route)**
**Range:**

- **Foam formulation (50 mcg/ml):** Apply 1 ml to the scalp nightly.
- **Dropper formulation, combined with GHK-Cu (10 mcg thymulin / 5 mg GHK-Cu per ml):** Apply 1 ml to the scalp nightly — this combo pairs thymulin's follicle-environment effects with GHK-Cu's collagen and circulation support.

**Frequency:**

- Nightly application, typically run in 30-day courses.

**Rationale:** Topical delivery keeps the effect localized to the scalp rather than attempting systemic thymic modulation, which isn't the goal for hair loss use.

---

🔹 **Cycle Length**
**Common cycle length:** 30-day courses, often repeated back-to-back since hair growth cycles are slow and results take months to assess.
**Off-cycle:** Not typically cycled off within a hair-loss protocol — more commonly just continued or reassessed at each 30-day mark.

---

🔹 **Optimal Dosing Window**

- Nightly, scalp-only application.
- Consistency matters more than timing — hair follicle response to any topical is a slow, cumulative process.

---

✅ **Educational Integration Example (topical)**

- **Example protocol:** 1 ml of the GHK-Cu/zinc thymulin combo dropper applied to the scalp nightly for 30 days, reassessed at the next course.
- Often used alongside other hair-focused topicals rather than as a standalone protocol.

---

🔹 **Stacking Strategy (Educational / Complementary)**
Since Zinc Thymulin's practical use here is scalp-localized, complementary stacking focuses on other hair- and skin-support compounds:
🔸 **Hair & Scalp Support:**

- **GHK-Cu:** Already commonly combined in the same formulation — collagen support and circulation complement thymulin's follicle-environment effect.

🔸 **Broader Thymic/Immune Family (for context, not typically stacked for hair loss specifically):**

- **Thymalin, Thymosin Alpha-1:** Other thymic peptides with systemic immune-modulating roles, distinct from the topical hair-loss use case covered here.

**Rationale:** For hair loss specifically, stacking logic centers on other topical scalp-support compounds rather than layering additional thymic peptides, since the mechanism relevant here is local, not systemic.
