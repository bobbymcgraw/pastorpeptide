### 🔹 **What is Pielotax?**

**Class:** Short peptide bioregulator (cytomax) derived from the kidney tissue of young calves, part of the Khavinson-style Russian peptide bioregulator family.
**Mechanism:**

- Stimulates cellular metabolism within the kidney, supporting renal tissue regeneration and general function.
- Carries antioxidant properties that regulate peroxide oxidation, protecting kidney cells from free-radical damage.
- Improves the kidney's ability to hold up under stress — hypertension, infection, and metabolic strain among them.
- Reduces renal inflammation, relevant to chronic kidney conditions and autoimmune-driven kidney stress.

**Primary focus:**

- Renal tissue support and function, relevant to chronic kidney disease, renal insufficiency, and gouty nephropathy.
- Secondary support for detoxification, waste filtration, and electrolyte balance.

🔹 **Oral Capsule Dosage (primary route)**
**Range:** Commonly dosed at **21.5 mg per capsule**, with **1–2 capsules taken 1–2 times daily**, generally for **30 days**.
**Typical protocol:** Some report best results taking 2 capsules in the morning, before eating, across the full 30-day window.
**Rationale:** The oral capsule format lets Pielotax act locally on kidney tissue through the digestive route without needing injection — standard for this peptide family.

---

🔹 **Repeat Courses**
**Range:** For chronic kidney conditions, courses may be repeated **every 3–6 months**, based on ongoing need.

---

🔹 **Cycle Length**
**Common cycle length:** 30 days per course, repeated every 3–6 months for chronic support.
**Off-cycle:** Cycled short courses with rest periods are standard rather than continuous daily use.

---

🔹 **Optimal Dosing Window**

- Morning dosing, before food, is a commonly reported preference for this particular bioregulator.
- Mild GI discomfort and temporary fatigue during the early phase of a course have been reported as the kidneys adapt to enhanced function and detox activity — described as short-lived, with no evidence of dependency.
