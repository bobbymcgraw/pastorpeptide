### 🔹 **What is Bronchogen?**

**Class:** Short peptide bioregulator derived from bronchial tissue, part of the Khavinson-style Russian organ-specific bioregulator line.
**Mechanism:**

- Promotes cellular repair and regeneration within bronchial and pulmonary tissue, aimed at supporting overall lung function.
- Dampens chronic airway inflammation, a mechanism relevant to bronchitis, asthma, and other inflammatory lung conditions.
- Reinforces local immune defenses in the lungs, contributing to greater resilience against respiratory infection.

**Primary focus:**

- Respiratory health and lung capacity, recovery support following infection, trauma, or surgery affecting the lungs, and general management of chronic respiratory conditions.

---

🔹 **Oral Capsule Dosage (primary route)**
**Range:** **20 mg per capsule**, typically **1–2 capsules taken 1–2 times daily**.
**Typical protocol:** A course of **20–30 days** is standard, with duration adjusted to baseline lung health and the specific goal of the protocol.
**Rationale:** Oral dosing is the standard route for this class of short bioregulator peptide, aiming to deliver an organ-specific signal to bronchial and pulmonary tissue without the need for inhaled or injectable delivery.

---

🔹 **Cycle Length**
**Common cycle length:** 20–30 days per course.
**Off-cycle:** As with other bioregulators in this family, courses are typically run periodically rather than continuously, with breaks between cycles.

---

🔹 **Optimal Dosing Window**

- No specific time-of-day requirement is emphasized in the source material; consistency across the day (1–2x dosing) is the more relevant factor.
- Some users report timing courses around seasonal respiratory stress (e.g., cold/flu season) or recovery periods following a respiratory illness.

---

✅ **Educational Integration Example (oral capsule)**

- **Example protocol:** 20 mg capsule, once or twice daily, for a 20–30 day course, considered around recovery from respiratory infection or as periodic seasonal support.
- Frequently discussed alongside Chonluten, given the overlapping lung-tissue focus of the two peptides.
