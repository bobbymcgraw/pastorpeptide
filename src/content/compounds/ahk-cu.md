### 🔹 **What is AHK-Cu (Copper Tripeptide-3)?**

**Class:** Copper-binding tripeptide (Alanyl-L-histidyl-L-lysine complexed with copper) — a close structural cousin of GHK-Cu, already covered on this site.
**Mechanism:**

- Like GHK-Cu, AHK-Cu chelates copper and uses that copper-binding capacity to influence cell signaling and tissue-remodeling activity — but the amino acid sequence is different (Ala-His-Lys instead of Gly-His-Lys), and that difference shifts its primary area of activity.
- Specifically studied for stimulating dermal papilla cells — the cells at the base of hair follicles that regulate the hair growth cycle and follicle activity.
- By supporting dermal papilla cell activity, AHK-Cu is theorized to help promote the follicle's transition into and maintenance of the active growth (anagen) phase.

**Primary focus:**

- Hair growth and scalp support, with a narrower, more hair-specific focus than GHK-Cu's broader skin, wound-healing, and anti-aging applications.
- See the site's **GHK-Cu** entry for the more extensively studied, broader-spectrum relative of this compound.

---

🔹 **Oral Dosage:** Not used; AHK-Cu is formulated and applied topically, not taken orally or systemically.

---

🔹 **Topical Dosage (only route in practical use)**
**Range:** Typically formulated at low percentage concentrations (commonly under 1%) in hair serums, scalp tonics, or leave-in treatments.
**Frequency:** Once daily, most often applied directly to the scalp.
**Rationale:** Topical application delivers the peptide directly to the follicle and dermal papilla environment without systemic exposure, consistent with how copper peptides are generally used for hair and skin applications.
**Notes:** Best applied to a clean, dry scalp for absorption; often combined with other hair-support actives (biotin, minoxidil, other copper peptides) in commercial formulations.

---

🔹 **Cycle Length**
**Common cycle length:** Hair growth cycles are slow, so use is generally ongoing over several months (often 3–6 months) before meaningful visual change would be expected, consistent with how hair-growth-focused topicals are typically used.
**Off-cycle:** No established washout period; use tends to be continuous rather than cycled.

---

✅ **Educational Integration Example (topical)**

- **Example protocol:** A scalp serum containing AHK-Cu applied nightly to the scalp, used consistently over a period of months as part of a broader hair-support routine.
- Frequently formulated alongside or discussed in comparison to **GHK-Cu**, which addresses skin and broader tissue repair rather than hair-follicle-specific activity.

---

🔹 **Stacking Strategy (Educational / Complementary)**
Since AHK-Cu's niche is hair-follicle support specifically, complementary approaches tend to focus on broader scalp and skin health:
🔸 **Copper Peptide Family:**

- **GHK-Cu:** Broader tissue repair, skin remodeling, and general anti-aging support that complements AHK-Cu's more targeted follicle activity.

🔸 **General Hair & Scalp Support:**

- **Biotin, topical minoxidil, other scalp-health actives:** Address hair growth through different, non-overlapping mechanisms.

**Rationale:** AHK-Cu's value is its specificity for the dermal papilla and hair cycle; stacking with GHK-Cu or other scalp-support actives broadens coverage without duplicating mechanisms.
