### 🔹 **What is IGF-1?**

**Class:** Naturally occurring peptide hormone, produced mainly in the liver in response to growth hormone stimulation.
**Mechanism:**

- Drives cellular proliferation, differentiation, and repair in muscle, bone, and cartilage.
- Enhances muscle protein synthesis, accelerating recovery and growth following training or injury.
- Improves insulin sensitivity and supports broader glucose metabolism.

**Primary focus:**

- Lean muscle development and accelerated recovery from injury.
- Bone density support, relevant to osteoporosis and fracture recovery contexts.
- General anti-aging and tissue longevity support as a downstream mediator of GH activity.

---

🔹 **Note on IGF-1 vs. IGF-1 LR3:** This entry covers native/recombinant IGF-1. **IGF-1 LR3**, covered separately on this site, is a modified long-acting analog engineered to resist binding proteins and remain active far longer in circulation. Native IGF-1 has a much shorter half-life and more closely mirrors the body's natural GH-IGF-1 signaling pattern.

---

🔹 **Oral Dosage:** Not viable; IGF-1 is a peptide hormone and does not survive oral administration.

---

🔹 **Injectable / Subcutaneous Dosage (only practical route)**
**Range:**

- Commonly referenced doses are **50–100 mcg daily**, often paired with GH or GH-secretagogue peptides for synergistic effect.

**Frequency:**

- Daily dosing during an active protocol.

**Rationale:** Because native IGF-1 clears quickly, consistent daily dosing is used to maintain a steady anabolic and repair signal.

---

🔹 **Cycle Length**
**Common cycle length:** 4–12 weeks depending on individual goals and response.
**Off-cycle:** Breaks allow reassessment of insulin sensitivity and glucose markers before resuming.

---

🔹 **Optimal Dosing Window**

- Post-workout dosing is commonly discussed to align with periods of heightened tissue repair demand.
- Consistency of timing matters more than a specific clock-time rule.

---

✅ **Educational Integration Example (injectable / subcutaneous)**

- **Example protocol:** 60 mcg subcutaneous daily for 6–8 weeks, paired with a GH secretagogue for synergistic anabolic support.
- Blood glucose monitoring is a reasonable part of any such protocol given IGF-1's insulin-like activity.

---

🔹 **Stacking Strategy (Educational / Complementary)**
Since IGF-1 sits downstream of the GH axis, complementary stacks tend to focus on amplifying the same anabolic and recovery pathways:
🔸 **GH-Axis Synergy:**

- **CJC-1295, Ipamorelin, Hexarelin:** GH secretagogues that increase endogenous GH, which in turn drives natural IGF-1 production — a complementary rather than redundant approach.

🔸 **Recovery & Tissue Repair:**

- **BPC-157, TB-500:** Support connective tissue and general recovery alongside IGF-1's proliferative effects on muscle and cartilage.

🔸 **Metabolic Monitoring Support:**

- Attention to glucose regulation is warranted given IGF-1's insulin-like signaling, particularly when stacked with other metabolic compounds.

**Rationale:** IGF-1 amplifies the anabolic output of the GH axis; stacking aims to support that same axis and its downstream repair demands without stacking multiple strong insulin-mimetic agents simultaneously.
