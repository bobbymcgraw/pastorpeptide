### 🔹 **What is Dihexa?**

**Class:** Synthetic oligopeptide (nootropic / neurogenic peptide).
**Mechanism:**

- Potent **HGF (hepatocyte growth factor) agonist** that promotes synaptogenesis, neurogenesis, and neuronal connectivity.
- Enhances cognitive function by supporting dendritic branching and synaptic density.
- Potentially improves memory formation, learning capacity, and neuroplasticity.

**Primary focus:** Cognitive enhancement (memory, learning, focus), neuroplasticity, neurorepair, and neuroregeneration.

---

🔹 **Oral Dosage (least common)**
**Range studied/used in research:** Extremely limited; oral bioavailability likely poor.
**Typical protocol:** Oral or sublingual use is largely anecdotal; most users prefer injectable forms for reliable absorption.
**Notes:** Oral administration is speculative and under-documented.

---

🔹 **Injectable / Subcutaneous Dosage (most common)**
**Range:**

- Common anecdotal dosing is **5–10 mg per week**, divided into daily or every-other-day subcutaneous injections.

**Frequency:** Daily or every-other-day dosing is typical for cognitive and neuroplasticity support.
**Rationale:** Microgram to low milligram dosing supports synaptogenesis while minimizing risk of overstimulation or off-target effects.

---

🔹 **Cycle Length**
**Common cycle length:** 4–6 weeks for neurocognitive or neuroregenerative support.
**Off-cycle:** 2–4 weeks to assess efficacy and avoid potential receptor downregulation.
**Note:** Duration is based on anecdotal practice and limited research; no formal clinical protocols exist.

---

🔹 **Optimal Dosing Window**

- Morning or early afternoon dosing is favored to align with peak cognitive activity.
- Avoid late evening doses in sensitive individuals due to potential overstimulation or sleep interference.

---

✅ **Educational Integration Example (injectable / subcutaneous)**

- **Example protocol:** 1–2 mg Dihexa daily via subcutaneous injection for 5–6 weeks, followed by a 2-week break.
- Pair with: Cognitive support supplements (omega-3 fatty acids, choline sources), adequate sleep, and mental stimulation.
