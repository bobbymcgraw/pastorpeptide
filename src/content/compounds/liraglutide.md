### 🔹 **What is Liraglutide?**

**Class:** Synthetic GLP-1 (glucagon-like peptide-1) receptor agonist — one of the original GLP-1 drugs, predating Semaglutide and Tirzepatide.
**Mechanism:**

- Activates GLP-1 receptors to boost insulin secretion and suppress glucagon release in a glucose-dependent manner.
- Slows gastric emptying, extending satiety signals and reducing overall caloric intake.
- Stabilizes post-meal glucose swings, improving glycemic control over time.

**Primary focus:**

- Type 2 diabetes management, particularly lowering HbA1c.
- Chronic weight management, marketed separately for this indication.
- Cardiovascular risk reduction in patients with Type 2 diabetes.

**Background — why GLP-1 analogues exist in the first place:**

- Native GLP-1 is a 30-amino-acid peptide hormone derived from the proglucagon gene, produced by intestinal enteroendocrine L-cells and by neurons in the brainstem (nucleus of the solitary tract) in response to food intake.
- The initial GLP-1(1-37) product is amidated and proteolytically cleaved into two equipotent active forms, GLP-1(7-36)amide and GLP-1(7-37).
- Endogenous GLP-1 is degraded almost immediately by the enzyme **DPP-4 (dipeptidyl peptidase-4)**, which is the entire reason long-acting synthetic analogues like liraglutide were engineered — to resist that rapid breakdown and remain active far longer than the native hormone.
- Notably, GLP-1's insulinotropic action (unlike the related incretin hormone GIP) remains preserved in Type 2 diabetics, which is part of why GLP-1-based drugs became the dominant pharmaceutical focus in this space.

---

🔹 **Oral Dosage:** Not available for liraglutide; unlike oral semaglutide (Rybelsus), liraglutide is only formulated for subcutaneous injection.

---

🔹 **Injectable / Subcutaneous Dosage (only route)**
**Range:**

- Initiated at **0.6 mg daily**, escalating gradually to a maximum of **3 mg daily**, based on tolerance and treatment goal.

**Frequency:**

- Daily injection is required — a key practical difference from the once-weekly dosing of Semaglutide and Tirzepatide.

**Rationale:** Liraglutide has a shorter half-life than newer GLP-1 agonists, which is why it requires daily rather than weekly administration.

---

🔹 **Cycle Length**
**Common cycle length:** Typically used as an ongoing daily therapy rather than in defined cycles, similar to other GLP-1 agonists.
**Off-cycle:** Discontinuation is generally guided by a healthcare provider based on treatment goals and tolerance.

---

🔹 **Optimal Dosing Window**

- Can be administered at any time of day, with or without food.
- Consistency in daily timing supports steadier appetite and glucose control.

---

✅ **Educational Integration Example (injectable)**

- **Example protocol:** Titration from 0.6 mg daily up through 1.2 mg, 1.8 mg, 2.4 mg, and ultimately 3 mg daily as tolerated, paired with dietary and activity changes.
- Because it requires daily injections, some users transition to weekly agonists like Semaglutide once available, trading daily dosing for convenience.

---

🔹 **Stacking Strategy (Educational / Complementary)**
Since liraglutide's core mechanisms are appetite suppression and glucose regulation, complementary support mirrors what's used with other GLP-1 agonists:
🔸 **Metabolic & Insulin Sensitivity Support:**

- **Berberine:** Supports insulin sensitivity and lipid metabolism alongside GLP-1 activity.

🔸 **Muscle Preservation:**

- **Creatine, adequate protein intake:** Helps offset lean mass loss that can accompany significant caloric reduction.

🔸 **Complementary Metabolic Peptides:**

- **Lipo-B, Lipo-C:** Lipotropic support for liver function and fat metabolism alongside GLP-1-driven weight loss.

**Rationale:** Liraglutide's appetite and glucose effects benefit from support that protects muscle mass and liver function during the caloric deficit it tends to produce.
