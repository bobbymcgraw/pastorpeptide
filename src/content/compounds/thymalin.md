### 🔹 **What is Thymalin?**

**Class:** Peptide bioregulator complex derived from thymus tissue.
**Mechanism:**

- Increases T-cell production and activity, strengthening adaptive immune defenses.
- Modulates cytokine signaling to balance immune responses rather than simply amplifying them, which helps temper inflammation.
- Supports tissue repair and regeneration, which is thought to aid recovery from infections and chronic illness.

**Primary focus:**

- Immune system support, particularly for individuals with weakened, aging, or compromised immune function.
- Chronic disease and recovery contexts — helping the body come back from illness and maintain resilience over time.

---

🔹 **Injectable (Intramuscular) Dosage — as described in the source literature**
**Range:**

- **Acute immune disorders/illness:** 5–20 mg per day for 3–10 days.
- **Preventive immune support:** 5–10 mg per day for 3–5 days.

**Rationale:** Thymalin is one of the older Khavinson-line bioregulators and is more commonly described in the literature as an injectable thymus extract complex rather than a standardized oral capsule — dosing above reflects that route as documented.

---

🔹 **Cycle Length**
**Common cycle length:** Short courses of 3–10 days depending on acute vs. preventive use.
**Off-cycle:** Periodic re-dosing is described for long-term immune maintenance rather than continuous daily use.

---

🔹 **Optimal Dosing Window**

- Daily dosing concentrated into a short, defined course rather than spread across an extended period.
- Positioned as a seasonal or as-needed immune "reset" rather than an everyday regimen.

---

✅ **Educational Integration Example**

- **Example protocol:** 10 mg per day for 5 days as a preventive immune course, repeated periodically (e.g., seasonally) for maintenance.
- Often discussed alongside **Thymogen** or **Vladonix** as part of a broader thymus/immune-support bioregulator category.

---

🔹 **Stacking Strategy (Educational / Complementary)**
Since Thymalin is squarely an **immune-modulating thymus bioregulator**, complementary pairings stay within immune and general recovery support:
🔸 **Immune-Focused Bioregulators:**

- **Thymogen, Vladonix:** Overlapping thymus/immune mechanisms; discussed together rather than layered redundantly.

🔸 **Recovery Support:**

- General tissue-repair-oriented compounds to complement the regenerative angle of immune recovery.

**Rationale:** Thymalin's mechanism is immune modulation and tissue repair via thymic signaling, so stacking logic favors reinforcing immune resilience rather than combining with unrelated hormonal bioregulators.
