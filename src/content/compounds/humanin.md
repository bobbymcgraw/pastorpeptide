### 🔹 **What is Humanin?**

**Class:** Mitochondrial-derived peptide (MDP), first identified in the hypothalamus.
**Mechanism:**

- Protects mitochondria from oxidative stress, helping preserve cellular energy output.
- Modulates cellular stress and inflammatory signaling pathways implicated in neurodegeneration.
- Associated in research with extended cellular lifespan and reduced age-related cellular damage.

**Primary focus:**

- Cellular longevity and protection against age-related decline.
- Neuroprotection, with exploratory interest in Alzheimer's and Parkinson's contexts.
- Cardiovascular tissue protection through reduced oxidative damage and inflammation.

---

🔹 **Oral Dosage**
**Range:**

- Available in capsule form, though oral bioavailability is considerably lower than injectable delivery.

**Notes:** Oral use is more of a convenience option than a reliable way to achieve meaningful systemic exposure.

---

🔹 **Injectable / Subcutaneous Dosage (most common for research use)**
**Range:**

- Typically **100–500 mcg per week**, adjusted based on the specific longevity or neuroprotective goal.

**Frequency:**

- Weekly dosing is the most commonly referenced schedule, sometimes split across the week.

**Rationale:** Subcutaneous delivery bypasses the bioavailability issues seen with oral administration, making it the preferred route when meaningful systemic exposure is the goal.

---

🔹 **Cycle Length**
**Common cycle length:** Often used in extended cycles given its longevity/cellular-health focus rather than short acute courses.
**Off-cycle:** Periodic reassessment is reasonable given how early-stage the human evidence base is.

---

🔹 **Optimal Dosing Window**

- No strong circadian rationale established; weekly consistency matters more than specific timing.

---

✅ **Educational Integration Example (injectable / subcutaneous)**

- **Example protocol:** 200 mcg subcutaneous 1–2 times weekly as part of a broader longevity-focused stack.
- Frequently discussed alongside other mitochondrial and cellular-health-focused compounds.

🔹 **Stacking Strategy (Educational / Complementary)**
Since Humanin's core value proposition is mitochondrial protection and cellular longevity, complementary stacks tend to focus on energy metabolism and antioxidant defense:
🔸 **Mitochondrial & Longevity Support:**

- **NAD⁺ precursors (NMN, NR):** Support the same mitochondrial energy pathways Humanin helps protect.
- **CoQ10, PQQ:** Mitochondrial cofactors that complement Humanin's protective action.

🔸 **Neuroprotective & Antioxidant Support:**

- **Glutathione:** Antioxidant support that reinforces Humanin's role in reducing oxidative cellular damage.
- **Epitalon:** Cellular longevity peptide that pairs conceptually with Humanin's anti-aging focus.

**Rationale:** Humanin protects the cell's energy infrastructure; stacking aims to reinforce mitochondrial resilience and reduce oxidative burden rather than duplicate its specific mechanism.
**Studied in Humans?** Limited — much of the evidence base is preclinical or early-stage, with human data still emerging.
