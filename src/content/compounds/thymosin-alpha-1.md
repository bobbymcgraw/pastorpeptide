## 🔹 What is Thymosin Alpha-1?

**Class:** Synthetic peptide fragment derived from thymosin fraction 5, originally isolated from the thymus gland.
**Mechanism:** Enhances **T-cell function**, increases **immune surveillance**, modulates **cytokine production**, and reduces **chronic inflammation**.
**Primary focus:** Immune optimization, anti-viral defense, cancer immunotherapy support, and reducing chronic low-grade inflammation.

---

## 🔹 Oral Dosage (least common)

**Range studied/used in research:** **Rarely oral** — poor bioavailability.
**Typical protocol:**

- 2–3 mg orally daily (experimental use, very limited data).
- Often paired with liposomal delivery systems to improve absorption.

**Notes:** Injectable form is far more reliable and widely used in research.

## 🔹 Injectable Dosage (subcutaneous – most common)

**Range:** **1.5–3 mg, 2–3x per week** (some protocols go up to 4.5 mg, 3x/week).
**Frequency:** Every other day or 2–3x weekly depending on immune status and research goals.
**Rationale:** Direct subQ bypasses degradation, ensuring strong immunomodulatory effect.

**Common cycle length:** **6–12 weeks** for immune system support.
**Off-cycle:** Often paused after protocol completion; may be reintroduced seasonally (e.g., during flu season) or post-infection recovery.
**Special note:** In chronic immune conditions, some research protocols use ongoing low-dose maintenance.
