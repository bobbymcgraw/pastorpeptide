### 🔹 **What is Tesofensine?**

**Class:** Small-molecule triple monoamine reuptake inhibitor (serotonin, norepinephrine, dopamine).
**Mechanism:**

- Inhibits reuptake of **dopamine, norepinephrine, and serotonin**, enhancing satiety and reducing appetite.
- Increases basal metabolic rate and may enhance lipolysis indirectly via sympathetic nervous system activation.
- Originally investigated for obesity management; also studied for potential cognitive and neuroprotective effects.

**Primary focus:** Appetite suppression, fat loss, metabolic rate increase, and potential improvements in body composition.

---

🔹 **Oral Dosage (only route)**
**Range studied/used in research:**

- Typically **0.25–1 mg per day**, titrated based on tolerance.
- Clinical trials for obesity used 0.5 mg daily with gradual titration up to 1 mg.

**Frequency:** Once daily, usually in the morning to minimize sleep disruption.
**Rationale:** Long half-life (~9–10 days) allows for once-daily dosing; morning administration helps align peak satiety effects with daytime meals.
**Notes:** Strong appetite suppression may occur; start at lower doses to gauge tolerance.

---

🔹 **Cycle Length**
**Common cycle length:** 4–12 weeks for fat loss protocols, sometimes longer under medical supervision.
**Off-cycle:** Breaks may be used to prevent receptor adaptation or appetite rebound; not well-studied.
**Note:** Long-term human safety data are limited; most information is from clinical trials or anecdotal use in the biohacking community.

---

🔹 **Optimal Dosing Window**

- **Morning dosing** preferred to avoid insomnia and optimize satiety during active hours.
- Avoid late afternoon or evening dosing, as it may cause sleep disturbances or overstimulation.

---

✅ **Educational Integration Example (oral)**

- Start at **0.25 mg daily** in the morning for 1 week to assess tolerance.
- Increase to **0.5 mg daily** if appetite suppression is insufficient and side effects are tolerable.
- Typical courses last 6–8 weeks, with breaks as needed.

**Pair with:** High-protein diet, resistance training, and cardiovascular activity to maximize fat loss while preserving lean mass.
