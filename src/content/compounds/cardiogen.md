### 🔹 **What is Cardiogen?**

**Class:** Short peptide bioregulator derived from cardiac tissue, part of the Khavinson-style Russian organ-specific bioregulator line.
**Mechanism:**

- Supports myocardial cell recovery, relevant to tissue healing after cardiac stress, cardiomyopathy, or infarction.
- Improves myocardial efficiency by supporting better oxygen utilization and energy production at the cellular level within heart tissue.
- Carries anti-inflammatory, cardioprotective effects that reduce inflammation across blood vessels and heart tissue.

**Primary focus:**

- Cardiac recovery support, improved cardiac output and efficiency, and general reduction of long-term cardiovascular risk (heart failure, arrhythmia, vascular complications).

🔹 **Oral Capsule Dosage (primary route)**
**Range:** **20 mg per capsule**, typically **1–2 capsules taken 1–2 times daily** with food.
**Typical protocol:** A **1-month course** is the standard reference point, with extension possible depending on cardiovascular health goals.
**Rationale:** As with the rest of this bioregulator family, the short peptide structure is designed for oral absorption and organ-specific signaling rather than requiring injectable delivery.
