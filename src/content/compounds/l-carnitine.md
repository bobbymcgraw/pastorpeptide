### 🔹 **What is L-Carnitine?**

**Class:** Nutrient / amino acid derivative.
**Mechanism:**

- Transports **long-chain fatty acids into mitochondria** for β-oxidation and ATP production.
- Supports **energy metabolism**, particularly during aerobic exercise.
- May reduce **exercise-induced fatigue, improve recovery**, and enhance fat utilization.

**Primary focus:**

- Fat metabolism and energy support.
- Exercise performance and recovery.
- Mitochondrial efficiency and potential neuroprotective effects.

---

🔹 **Oral Dosage**
**Range studied/used in research:**

- Typical daily dosing: **500–2,000 mg** orally.
- Can be divided into 1–2 doses per day.

**Notes:** Oral bioavailability is moderate (~15–20%). Best taken with meals containing carbohydrates to enhance uptake via insulin-mediated transport.

---

🔹 **Injectable / Subcutaneous Dosage (optional, for enhanced delivery)**
**Range:**

- Common doses in clinical/research contexts: **250–500 mg per injection**, 2–3x weekly.
- Often part of **fat-loss or energy-support blends** (e.g., Lipo-C, Super Shred).

**Rationale:** Injectable delivery bypasses GI limitations and increases plasma availability for rapid mitochondrial uptake.

---

🔹 **Cycle Length**
**Common cycle length:** 4–12 weeks, often used continuously for performance, energy, or fat metabolism support.
**Off-cycle:** Not strictly required; may take breaks to assess efficacy or rotate with other mitochondrial support compounds.

---

🔹 **Optimal Dosing Window**

- **Morning or pre-workout:** Enhances energy availability and fat utilization during exercise.
- **With meals:** Oral dosing benefits from insulin-mediated uptake.

---

✅ **Educational Integration Example**

- **Oral:** 1,000 mg L-Carnitine daily in 2 divided doses, morning and pre-workout.
- **Injectable:** 500 mg subcutaneous 2x per week as part of an energy/fat metabolism blend.
- Pair with: Adequate hydration, mitochondrial cofactors (CoQ10, PQQ), and aerobic or endurance training for maximal effect.

---

🔹 **Stacking Strategy (Educational / Complementary)**
Since L-Carnitine **supports mitochondrial fatty acid oxidation**, complementary stacks focus on **energy metabolism, recovery, and fat loss**:
🔸 **Metabolic & Mitochondrial Support:**

- **NAD⁺ precursors (NMN, NR), PQQ, CoQ10:** Support mitochondrial efficiency.
- **AICAR:** Enhances AMPK signaling and mitochondrial biogenesis.

🔸 **Fat Loss / Performance:**

- **Injectable blends (Lipo-C, Super Shred):** Combine L-Carnitine with methionine, choline, B12, and MIC for enhanced fat metabolism.
- **Cardiovascular or endurance training:** Improves utilization of fatty acids as energy substrate.

🔸 **Recovery Support:**

- **Amino acids, BCAAs, and antioxidants:** Reduce exercise-induced oxidative stress and muscle fatigue.

**Rationale:** L-Carnitine acts as a mitochondrial fatty acid transporter; stacking aims to **enhance energy availability, fat oxidation, and recovery** without overlapping mechanisms.
