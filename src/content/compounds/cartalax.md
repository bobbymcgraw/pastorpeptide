### 🔹 **What is Cartalax?**

**Class:** Short peptide bioregulator derived from cartilage tissue, part of the Khavinson-style Russian organ-specific bioregulator line.
**Mechanism:**

- Stimulates chondrocyte activity, the cells responsible for building and maintaining cartilage.
- Reduces joint inflammation, a mechanism relevant to arthritis, osteoarthritis, and other inflammatory joint conditions.
- Boosts type II collagen synthesis, a key structural protein for joint strength, flexibility, and durability.

**Primary focus:**

- Joint mobility and pain reduction, recovery support following joint injury or surgery, and slowing long-term cartilage breakdown.

---

🔹 **Oral Capsule Dosage (primary route)**
**Range:** **21.5 mg per capsule**, typically **1–2 capsules taken 1–2 times daily** with food.
**Typical protocol:** Courses commonly run **10–30 days**, often repeated periodically to maintain joint health over time.
**Rationale:** The short peptide structure allows for oral dosing aimed at a cartilage-specific signal rather than needing intra-articular injection.

---

🔹 **Cycle Length**
**Common cycle length:** 10–30 days, frequently used as recurring short cycles rather than a single long course.
**Off-cycle:** Cycling on and off is the more typical pattern reported for long-term joint health maintenance.
