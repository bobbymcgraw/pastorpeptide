Tissue repair peptides are short chains of amino acids that act as signaling molecules in the body to promote healing and regeneration.1 They mimic naturally occurring peptides and can accelerate the body's repair processes, especially in musculoskeletal injuries and skin wounds.2 
They work by influencing key aspects of the healing cascade, including:

- **Cell Migration and Proliferation:** They guide and stimulate the movement and growth of essential cells, like fibroblasts, to the injury site.3
- **Angiogenesis:** They promote the formation of new blood vessels, which is critical for delivering oxygen and nutrients to damaged tissues.4
- **Collagen Synthesis:** They help in the production and proper organization of collagen, the main structural protein in connective tissues, leading to stronger, more functional repaired tissue.5
- **Inflammation Modulation:** They can reduce inflammation, which helps alleviate pain and prevents it from impeding the healing process.6

### Key Types of Tissue Repair Peptides 🩹

There are several types of peptides used for tissue repair, each with a unique mechanism of action.7

#### **BPC-157 (Body Protection Compound-157)**

Derived from a protein found in human gastric juice, **BPC-157** is a stable peptide known for its regenerative and anti-inflammatory properties.8 It is often called the "body protection compound" because it has shown promise in healing various tissues, from the gastrointestinal tract to muscles, tendons, ligaments, and bones.9

- **Mechanism:** BPC-157 promotes the expression of growth hormone receptors and several pathways involved in cell growth and angiogenesis.10 It can accelerate tendon and ligament healing by stimulating fibroblast migration and collagen production.11

#### **TB-500 (Thymosin Beta-4)**

A synthetic version of the naturally occurring peptide **thymosin beta-4**, which is found in nearly all human and animal cells.12 TB-500 is known for its ability to promote systemic healing and regeneration, especially in soft tissue injuries.13

- **Mechanism:** TB-500 works by regulating **actin**, a protein crucial for cell structure and movement.14 It promotes the migration of cells to the injury site and stimulates the formation of new blood vessels, improving blood flow and delivering healing agents to the damaged tissue. It also has potent anti-inflammatory effects and may reduce scar tissue formation.15

#### **GHK-Cu (Copper Tripeptide-1)**

A naturally occurring peptide complex that binds with copper ions.16 Its concentration decreases with age, which is why it's a popular ingredient in anti-aging skincare. GHK-Cu is a potent wound healer and has a long-documented history in skin regeneration.17

- **Mechanism:** GHK-Cu stimulates the synthesis of **collagen** and **elastin**, key components for skin elasticity and strength.18 It also acts as an antioxidant, reduces inflammation, and promotes the proliferation of cells like fibroblasts and keratinocytes, which are essential for wound closure and skin barrier restoration.

#### **α-MSH (Alpha-Melanocyte-Stimulating Hormone)**

A peptide hormone with potent anti-inflammatory and protective effects.19 While it's best known for its role in pigmentation, its regenerative properties are being studied for therapeutic use.20 A derivative of this, the tripeptide KPV, is also being investigated for its anti-inflammatory actions without the pigmentary effects.21

- **Mechanism:** α-MSH can modulate inflammatory responses by suppressing the activation of key inflammatory pathways.22 It also has direct protective effects on cells and tissues.
