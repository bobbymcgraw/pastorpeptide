### 🔹 **What is Pancragen?**

**Class:** Short peptide bioregulator (cytomax) derived from pancreatic tissue, part of the Khavinson-style Russian peptide bioregulator family.
**Mechanism:**

- Signals pancreatic cells — including insulin-producing beta cells — toward repair and functional recovery.
- Supports digestive enzyme output, which plays into nutrient absorption and overall metabolic efficiency.
- Helps regulate insulin production and glucose handling as part of its broader metabolic role.

**Primary focus:**

- Pancreatic tissue support relevant to blood sugar regulation, insulin balance, and recovery from pancreatic inflammation.
- Secondary support for digestive function and nutrient absorption.

---

🔹 **Oral Capsule Dosage (primary route)**
**Range:** Commonly dosed at **100 mcg per capsule**, with **1–2 capsules taken 1–2 times daily**.
**Typical protocol:** A standard **30-day course**, sometimes repeated in cycles depending on ongoing metabolic support needs.
**Rationale:** As a short peptide bioregulator, Pancragen is built for oral use, acting locally on pancreatic tissue signaling without requiring injection.

---

🔹 **Cycle Length**
**Common cycle length:** 30 days per course.
**Off-cycle:** Cycled short courses with breaks between them, rather than continuous daily use, is the standard approach for this category.

---

🔹 **Optimal Dosing Window**

- Consistency with meals may help minimize the mild digestive discomfort some report during the initial adaptation phase.
- Anyone managing diabetes or blood sugar sensitivity should monitor glucose closely during a course, since Pancragen's mechanism touches insulin regulation directly.

---

✅ **Educational Integration Example (oral capsule)**

- **Example protocol:** 100 mcg capsule, 1–2 capsules daily, for 30 days, used as part of a broader metabolic-support routine.
- Often discussed alongside other metabolic and digestive-support bioregulators.

🔹 **Stacking Strategy (Educational / Complementary)**
Since Pancragen is focused on **pancreatic repair, digestive enzyme support, and glucose regulation**, complementary stacking tends to lean toward metabolic and hepatic support:
🔸 **Metabolic Support:**

- **Ovagen, Svetinorm:** Liver-focused bioregulators with overlapping metabolic and glucose-regulation angles.

**Rationale:** Pancragen targets pancreatic tissue and glucose metabolism specifically; stacking aims to **reinforce broader metabolic health** across complementary organ systems.
