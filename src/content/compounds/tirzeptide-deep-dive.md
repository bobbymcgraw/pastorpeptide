How It Works

Tirzepatide (Mounjaro / Zepbound) is a **dual incretin receptor agonist** that activates both the **GLP-1** and **GIP** receptors. By targeting these two key metabolic pathways together, it regulates appetite, improves insulin sensitivity, controls blood glucose, and promotes fat loss.

- **GLP-1 →** slows gastric emptying, reduces appetite, enhances insulin secretion, and improves blood sugar control.
- **GIP →** boosts insulin response in a glucose-dependent way, improves nutrient partitioning, enhances fat metabolism, and may provide additional cardiovascular protection.

This dual action makes Tirzepatide more powerful than GLP-1–only therapies like semaglutide. Unlike Retatrutide, however, Tirzepatide does **not** activate the glucagon receptor, so it doesn’t directly increase energy expenditure or fat oxidation.

What To Expect

### Benefits

- **Weight loss:** In large Phase 3 SURMOUNT trials, patients lost up to ~20% of body weight at 72 weeks (15mg dose). Even lower doses (5–10mg) produced substantial loss (~15–18%).
- **Superior efficacy:** In head-to-head studies, Tirzepatide outperformed semaglutide in both weight reduction and HbA1c improvement.
- **Glucose control:** HbA1c reduced by up to 2.3% in T2D patients; many prediabetics reverted to normal glucose tolerance.
- **Liver health:** Trials show significant reductions in liver fat (NAFLD/MASLD), tied to both weight loss and improved insulin sensitivity.

**Cardiometabolic benefits:** Lowers blood pressure, improves lipid profile (↓ LDL, triglycerides; ↑ HDL), with ongoing CV outcome studies showing promising early signals.

### Common Side Effects

- **Gastrointestinal:** nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, constipation, abdominal discomfort (dose-dependent, often improving with time).
- **Heart rate:** small mean increase (similar to GLP-1s, usually not clinically significant).Risks & Cautions

## Risks & Cautions

- **Gallbladder issues:** class-related risk during rapid weight loss.
- **Pancreatitis / thyroid C-cell risk:** similar caution as with other GLP-1 therapies.
- **Cardiac monitoring:** advisable for those with underlying heart disease until long-term CV data are complete.

Dosing & Protocols

Tirzepatide is FDA-approved, so dosing is standardized. The key is **slow titration** to minimize GI side effects.

### Standard FDA Schedule

- Weeks 1–4: 2.5mg weekly (for tolerance, not weight loss)
- Weeks 5–8: 5mg weekly
- Weeks 9–12: 7.5mg weekly
- Weeks 13–16: 10mg weekly
- Weeks 17–20: 12.5mg weekly
- Weeks 21+: 15mg weekly (max dose)

### Minimal-Increase Schedule (for sensitive patients)

- Weeks 1–8: 2.5mg weekly
- Weeks 9–16: 5mg weekly
- Weeks 17+: only increase if weight loss plateaus for 4+ weeks despite consistent lifestyle factors

### Important Considerations

- **Lowest effective dose:** Many patients achieve excellent results at 5–10mg; 15mg provides modest additional benefit but more side effects.
- **Injection timing:** Once weekly, any time of day, with or without food. Some split doses into twice-weekly injections for smoother tolerance.
- **Monitoring:** HbA1c, liver enzymes, lipid panel, and optional DEXA scans for fat vs lean mass changes.
