### 🔹 **What is KPV?**

**Class:** Tripeptide (Lys-Pro-Val), derived from alpha-Melanocyte Stimulating Hormone (α-MSH) — the anti-inflammatory activity of α-MSH is attributed specifically to these three N-terminal amino acids.
**Mechanism:**

- Exhibits **anti-inflammatory and immunomodulatory effects** via inhibition of NF-κB signaling.
- Reduces pro-inflammatory cytokine production (TNF-α, IL-6) in tissues.
- Supports **gut health, wound healing, and systemic inflammation control**.

**Primary focus:**

- Anti-inflammatory support, tissue repair, gastrointestinal health, and recovery from chronic inflammation or localized injury.
- Often included in peptide blends for **skin, connective tissue, or systemic inflammation reduction**.

---

🔹 **Oral Dosage:** Not practical; degraded in the GI tract. Only injectable, subcutaneous, or topical administration is effective.

**Clinical dosing options in practice:**

- **Capsule:** KPV 500 mcg capsules, one capsule PO twice daily.
- **Topical:** KPV (2 mg/g) + Vitamin D3 (1,000 IU/g) compounded topical cream, applied via topi-click dispenser.

---

🔹 **Injectable / Subcutaneous Dosage (most common)**
**Range:**

- Typical dosing: **250–500 µg per injection**, daily or every other day depending on tissue target and severity of inflammation.

**Frequency:**

- Often administered **daily** for ongoing anti-inflammatory or healing protocols.

**Rationale:** Microgram-level dosing is sufficient due to potent anti-inflammatory activity; subcutaneous delivery ensures systemic or local tissue availability.

---

🔹 **Topical Dosage (for localized inflammation or skin health)**

- Typical topical formulations: **0.1–0.5% KPV cream or gel** applied once or twice daily to affected area.
- May be combined with **GH or regenerative peptides** (e.g., GHK-Cu, BPC-157) for enhanced local tissue repair.

**Notes:** Topical application targets local inflammatory pathways, useful for skin, joints, or localized injuries.

---

🔹 **Cycle Length**
**Common cycle length:** 4–8 weeks for anti-inflammatory, regenerative, or gut health purposes.
**Off-cycle:** 2–4 weeks to assess response and prevent potential receptor habituation.

---

🔹 **Optimal Dosing Window**

- **Injectable:** Morning or evening depending on systemic anti-inflammatory goals.
- **Topical:** Morning and/or evening for targeted tissue recovery.

⚠️ **Safety Notes**

- Unlike alpha-MSH, KPV is **not reported to cause skin pigmentation** — a common concern with melanocortin-pathway compounds.
- Reported safe and effective orally at recommended doses, with no notable side effects.
