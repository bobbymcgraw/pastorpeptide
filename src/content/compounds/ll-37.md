### 🔹 **What is LL-37?**

**Class:** Antimicrobial host defense peptide (human cathelicidin). LL-37 is a 37-amino-acid antimicrobial peptide derived from the human cathelicidin protein hCAP18.
**Mechanism:**

- Exhibits **broad-spectrum antimicrobial activity** against bacteria, viruses, and fungi.
- Modulates the **immune response** by promoting chemotaxis, angiogenesis, and wound healing.
- Enhances **tissue repair** through immunomodulatory and regenerative signaling pathways.

**Primary focus:**

- Wound healing, tissue regeneration, and antimicrobial defense.
- Potential therapeutic applications in **skin infections, chronic wounds, and immune support**.

---

🔹 **Oral Dosage:** Not effective; peptide is degraded in the GI tract. Only **injectable, subcutaneous, or topical** routes are practical.

---

🔹 **Injectable / Subcutaneous Dosage (most common)**
**Range:**

- Typical dosing in research: **50–200 µg per injection**, 1–2x daily depending on severity and target tissue.

**Frequency:**

- Often administered **daily** for acute wound or infection management.

**Rationale:** Microgram-level dosing ensures antimicrobial and regenerative effects without systemic toxicity.

---

🔹 **Topical Dosage (for skin or localized wounds)**

- Typical topical formulations: **0.05–0.2% LL-37 cream or gel**, applied 1–2x daily to the affected area.
- Useful for chronic wounds, ulcers, or localized infection/inflammation.

**Notes:** Topical administration targets local tissue repair and antimicrobial activity directly at the site of injury.

---

🔹 **Cycle Length**
**Common cycle length:** 2–6 weeks depending on wound healing or antimicrobial needs.
**Off-cycle:** 1–2 weeks to monitor healing and tissue response.

---

🔹 **Optimal Dosing Window**

- **Injectable:** Morning or early afternoon to align with tissue repair cycles.
- **Topical:** Morning and/or evening for targeted wound management.

---

✅ **Educational Integration Example**
