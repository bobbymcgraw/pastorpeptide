### 🔹 **What is MT-1** Melanotan 1 (Afamelanotide) **?**

**Class:** Synthetic melanocortin peptide analog.
**Mechanism:**

- Binds to **melanocortin receptors (primarily MC1R)** in the skin.
- Stimulates **melanin production**, leading to skin pigmentation and tanning.
- May also influence **satiety and libido** via central melanocortin pathways.

**Primary focus:**

- Skin pigmentation and UV-independent tanning.
- Potential support for **appetite regulation and libido enhancement**.

---

🔹 **Oral Dosage:** Not effective; degraded in the GI tract. Only **subcutaneous injection** is practical.

---

🔹 **Injectable / Subcutaneous Dosage (most common)**
**Range:**

- Typical dosing: **0.25–1 mg per injection**, 3–5x weekly.

**Frequency:**

- Administered **subcutaneously**, often alternating injection sites.

**Rationale:** Microgram-to-milligram doses are sufficient for MC1R activation and melanin production.

---

🔹 **Cycle Length**
**Common cycle length:** 4–12 weeks for progressive tanning or skin adaptation.
**Off-cycle:** 2–4 weeks breaks may be used to prevent receptor desensitization or allow melanin stabilization.

---

🔹 **Optimal Dosing Window**

- **Morning or early afternoon** preferred to coordinate with UV exposure (if used).
- Avoid late-evening dosing in sensitive individuals; anecdotal reports suggest occasional insomnia in rare cases.

---

✅ **Educational Integration Example**

- **Example protocol:** 0.5 mg subcutaneous injection 3x per week for 8 weeks.
- Pair with: Controlled sun exposure or UV-free tanning, adequate hydration, and skin care for optimal pigmentation results.

---

🔹 **Stacking Strategy (Educational / Complementary)**

- **GLP-1 agonists or L-Carnitine:** May complement melanocortin-mediated satiety or energy balance pathways.
