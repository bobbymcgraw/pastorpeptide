### 🔹 **What is PNC-27?**

**Class:** Chimeric ("designer") research peptide — a fusion of two distinct functional pieces rather than a naturally occurring or single-mechanism compound.
**Mechanism:**

- Combines a fragment derived from the **p53 tumor-suppressor protein** with **penetratin**, a cell-penetrating peptide sequence that helps the construct cross cell membranes.
- The p53-derived portion is theorized to target **HDM-2** (also called MDM2), a protein that many cancer cells overexpress on their outer cell membrane — a notable contrast to healthy cells, which mostly keep HDM-2 confined inside the cell rather than displaying it on the surface.
- PNC-27 is theorized to bind this surface-expressed HDM-2 and form pores in the cancer cell's membrane, causing the cell to lyse (rupture) and die — a direct, mechanical mode of cell destruction rather than a signaling-based one.
- Because healthy cells generally don't display HDM-2 on their surface, the proposed selectivity is that PNC-27 would spare normal tissue while targeting cells that do.

**Research status — read this before anything else:** PNC-27 is an experimental anticancer research compound studied in **in-vitro (cell culture) and animal models**. It has not gone through human clinical trials, and there is no established, validated human use pattern for it. This is fundamentally different from something like BPC-157 or other compounds on this site that, while also not FDA-approved, do have a substantial base of real-world human use and anecdotal protocol data behind them. PNC-27 does not. People are not meaningfully self-administering this compound in wellness or biohacking contexts, and we're not going to write as if they are.

**Primary focus (research context only):** Investigational targeted cancer cell lysis, studied at the preclinical level.
