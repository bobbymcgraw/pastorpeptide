### 🔹 **What is Calcitonin?**

**Class:** Naturally occurring peptide hormone secreted by the thyroid gland; salmon-derived calcitonin is the form most used clinically.
**Mechanism:**

- Suppresses osteoclast activity, slowing the rate at which bone tissue is broken down.
- Promotes renal excretion of excess calcium, helping regulate serum calcium levels.
- Exerts a notable analgesic effect on bone-related pain, independent of its calcium-regulating action.

**Primary focus:**

- Bone health conditions such as osteoporosis, Paget's disease, and hypercalcemia.
- Pain relief in acute bone events like vertebral compression fractures.
- Short-to-medium-term calcium regulation in hypercalcemia of malignancy.

---

🔹 **Oral Dosage:** Not effective; calcitonin is a peptide hormone and does not survive oral administration intact.

---

🔹 **Nasal Spray Dosage (most common route)**
**Range:**

- Standard osteoporosis dosing is **200 IU daily** via intranasal spray.

**Frequency:**

- Once daily, alternating nostrils is a common practice pattern.

**Rationale:** Nasal delivery is favored clinically for convenience and consistent absorption compared to injection, particularly for long-term osteoporosis management.

---

🔹 **Injectable / Subcutaneous Dosage**
**Range:**

- **50–100 IU daily**, adjusted to the condition being treated (hypercalcemia, Paget's disease, fracture-related pain).

**Frequency:**

- Daily dosing during active treatment, with reassessment based on calcium levels and symptom response.

**Rationale:** Injectable dosing allows more direct, titratable control in situations where nasal delivery isn't sufficient, such as acute hypercalcemia.

---

🔹 **Cycle Length**
**Common cycle length:** Osteoporosis protocols are often long-term/maintenance in nature rather than short cycles.
**Off-cycle:** Periodic reassessment of bone density and calcium markers is standard practice with prolonged use.

---

🔹 **Optimal Dosing Window**

- Nasal spray is typically used at the same time daily for consistency.
- No strong circadian rationale exists here; consistency matters more than specific timing.

---

✅ **Educational Integration Example (nasal spray)**

- **Example protocol:** 200 IU intranasal calcitonin daily, used long-term for osteoporosis management alongside calcium and vitamin D support.
- Commonly discussed alongside other bone-health-focused nutrients rather than GH or repair peptides.

---

🔹 **Stacking Strategy (Educational / Complementary)**
Since calcitonin's primary role is bone metabolism and calcium regulation, complementary support tends to focus on skeletal health broadly:
🔸 **Bone & Mineral Support:**

- **Calcium and Vitamin D:** Foundational cofactors for any bone-remodeling protocol involving calcitonin.
- **Collagen peptides:** Support the connective tissue matrix alongside calcitonin's effect on bone turnover.

🔸 **Recovery & Regeneration Support:**

- **BPC-157, TB-500:** Complementary tissue repair support in fracture recovery contexts, working through different mechanisms than calcitonin's calcium/osteoclast pathway.

**Rationale:** Calcitonin's mechanism is narrow and calcium-focused, so stacking logic centers on reinforcing skeletal structure and healing rather than amplifying its own pathway.
