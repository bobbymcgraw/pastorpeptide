### 🔹 **What is Retinalamin?**

**Class:** Short peptide bioregulator (cytomax) derived from retinal tissue, part of the Khavinson-style Russian peptide bioregulator family.
**Mechanism:**

- Stimulates regeneration of retinal cells, relevant to macular degeneration, diabetic retinopathy, and retinitis pigmentosa.
- Protects against oxidative stress at the retinal level, working to slow cell degeneration.
- Reduces ocular inflammation, relevant to conditions like uveitis and chronic retinal stress.

**Primary focus:**

- Retinal regeneration and vision support, particularly in the context of degenerative eye disease.
- Slowing progression of vision loss tied to diabetes, aging, or oxidative stress on the retina.

---

🔹 **Intramuscular Injection (primary route in research)**
**Range:** **5–10 mg per day** for **5–10 days**, scaled to individual vision-health needs.
**Rationale:** Retinalamin's research history leans heavily on the intramuscular route rather than oral capsule use, distinguishing it from many other bioregulators in this family.

---

🔹 **Parabulbar Injection (specialized route)**
**Notes:** A more targeted injection delivered near the eye itself. Given the proximity to the optic nerve and retina, this route should only be performed by a qualified medical professional and is not a self-directed option.

---

🔹 **Cycle Length**
**Common cycle length:** 5–10 days per course.
**Off-cycle:** Short, defined courses with breaks between them are standard, consistent with other bioregulators in this family.

---

🔹 **Optimal Dosing Window**

- Daily dosing across the short course window is standard; no particular time-of-day preference is emphasized.
- Mild injection-site irritation and temporary visual adjustments have been reported as cells adapt to improved function — generally described as short-lived.

---

✅ **Educational Integration Example (intramuscular)**

- **Example protocol:** 5–10 mg intramuscular daily for 7–10 days, used as a short, medically supervised course for retinal support.
- Often discussed alongside Normoftal given the shared retinal-tissue focus, though the two differ in route (injectable vs. oral).

---

🔹 **Stacking Strategy (Educational / Complementary)**
Since Retinalamin is focused on **retinal regeneration, neuroprotection, and anti-inflammatory support**, complementary stacking tends to lean toward other vision and neuroprotective compounds:
🔸 **Vision-Focused Bioregulators:**

- **Normoftal:** Overlapping retinal-tissue focus via the oral capsule route, often discussed as a complementary or alternative option.

🔸 **General Neuroprotective Support:**

- **Pinealon:** Broader neuroprotective bioregulator that may pair conceptually given the shared neuroprotective mechanism applied to different tissue.

**Rationale:** Retinalamin targets retinal tissue through a more clinically directed injectable route; stacking aims to **reinforce neuroprotection and anti-inflammatory support** across complementary approaches.
