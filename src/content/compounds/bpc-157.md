BPC-157, or Body Protection Compound-157, is a synthetic peptide composed of 15 amino acids that was originally isolated from a protein found in human gastric juice. It has garnered significant attention for its remarkable regenerative and anti-inflammatory properties, particularly in animal studies. The peptide works by promoting the formation of new blood vessels (angiogenesis), which is essential for delivering oxygen and nutrients to injured tissue, and by upregulating growth factors and pathways crucial for cell migration and tissue repair. This dual action has shown promise in accelerating the healing of a wide range of tissues, including muscles, tendons, ligaments, bones, and the gastrointestinal tract. While BPC-157 is not currently approved for human use by any major regulatory agencies, it is being used in some alternative medicine and wellness circles for its potential to speed up recovery from injuries.

---

### 🔹 **What is BPC-157?**

**Class:** Synthetic peptide—Body Protective Compound (BPC) fragment 157, derived from a naturally occurring protein in human gastric juice. Human BPC is naturally found in gastric juices, made in the lining of the stomach.
**Mechanism:** Promotes angiogenesis (formation of new blood vessels), collagen synthesis, and tissue repair. Modulates growth factors such as VEGF, accelerates wound healing, and protects organs from stress-induced damage. Supports tendon, ligament, muscle, and gut repair while mitigating inflammation and oxidative stress. In the GI tract specifically, BPC-157 acts as an antinuclear peptidergic agent relevant to GERD, is cytoprotective, improves nitric oxide (NO), helps improve GI mucosal integrity, and decreases gastric side effects of NSAIDs and alcohol.
**Primary focus:** Tissue repair and regeneration, injury recovery (muscle, tendon, ligament, gut), anti-inflammatory effects, joint health, and gut integrity.

🔹 **Oral / Sublingual Dosage**
**Range studied/used in research:** Limited human oral data; mostly animal studies. Oral administration is possible due to stability in gastric juice.
**Typical protocol:** Oral capsules or sublingual formulations range from 200–500 µg daily in anecdotal reports, but clinical standards are not established.
**Notes:** Sublingual/oral use is considered convenient but may have variable absorption; subcutaneous or topical routes are more predictable for localized tissue repair.

🔹 **Injectable / Subcutaneous / Intramuscular Dosage (most common)**
**Range:** Commonly used at 200–500 µg daily subcutaneously or intramuscularly, sometimes split into 2 doses for sustained tissue exposure.
**Frequency:** Once or twice daily depending on injury severity or research application.
**Rationale:** Direct injection near injured tissue (localized) or systemically (general healing) ensures optimal bioavailability and accelerates repair mechanisms.
