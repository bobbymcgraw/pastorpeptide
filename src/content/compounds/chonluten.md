### 🔹 **What is Chonluten?**

**Class:** Short peptide bioregulator derived from lung tissue, part of the Khavinson-style Russian organ-specific bioregulator line.
**Mechanism:**

- Stimulates repair and regeneration in lung parenchyma and bronchial tissue, contributing to improved pulmonary function.
- Reduces inflammatory processes in the lungs and airways, a mechanism relevant to COPD, asthma, and chronic bronchitis.
- Reinforces local immune protection within the lungs, lowering infection risk and improving overall respiratory resilience.

**Primary focus:**

- Oxygen exchange, lung capacity, and breathing efficiency, recovery support for chronic respiratory conditions, and general management of airway inflammation.

---

🔹 **Oral Capsule Dosage (primary route)**
**Range:** **21.5 mg per capsule**, typically **1–2 capsules taken 1–2 times daily** with food.
**Typical protocol:** Courses commonly run **10–30 days**, often repeated for ongoing pulmonary support.
**Rationale:** The short peptide structure allows oral dosing to target lung tissue specifically without requiring inhaled or injectable delivery.

---

🔹 **Cycle Length**
**Common cycle length:** 10–30 days per course.
**Off-cycle:** Repeat cycling over time is the more typical pattern for long-term pulmonary maintenance, rather than continuous indefinite use.

---

🔹 **Optimal Dosing Window**

- Taken **with food** to support absorption.
- No specific circadian timing is emphasized in the source material beyond daily consistency.

---

✅ **Educational Integration Example (oral capsule)**

- **Example protocol:** 21.5 mg capsule, once or twice daily with meals, for a 10–30 day course, considered around chronic respiratory condition management or recovery from a lung infection.
- Frequently discussed alongside Bronchogen, given the overlapping lung-tissue focus of the two peptides.

🔹 **Stacking Strategy (Educational / Complementary)**
Since Chonluten is centered on **lung tissue regeneration and airway inflammation**, complementary stacking tends to focus on the broader respiratory and immune systems:
🔸 **Respiratory-Focused Bioregulators:**

- **Bronchogen:** Bronchial-tissue bioregulator with a closely related focus, sometimes used interchangeably or in sequence depending on whether bronchial or broader lung parenchyma is the priority.

🔸 **Immune Support:**

- **Thymogen, Crystagen:** General immune-modulating bioregulators that may complement Chonluten's local respiratory immune effects.

**Rationale:** Chonluten targets lung tissue specifically; stacking aims to **reinforce respiratory resilience and systemic immune defense** around it.
