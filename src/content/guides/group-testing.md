**🗳️ Step 1: Group Formation & Voting**

- Buyers in a dedicated group (e.g., a Telegram channel or Discord) form a testing pool for a given batch or vendor.
- Members vote or poll on which types of testing the group wants, balancing thoroughness versus cost.
- Common testing options include:
  - Peptide identity (LC‑MS or MS/MS)
  - Purity by HPLC
  - Endotoxin (LAL)
  - Sterility screening
  - Heavy metals, residual solvents, amino acid analysis

Each added test increases cost—so the group decides collectively how deep the analysis should go.
**💵 Step 2: Cost Breakdown by Test (Typical Ranges)**
Testing providers like those in the US or Europe offer customized pricing based on scope. According to labs like ResolveMass:

- HPLC purity and Mass spec analysis: $250–$450
- Amino acid sequencing or profiling: $150–$350
- Endotoxin or sterility tests: often $250+ each for microbiological assays

So if a group wants identity + purity + endotoxin + sterility, total cost can approach $1,200 to $1,500 or more depending on sample complexity or fast turnaround.
**👥 Step 3: Pooling Contributions**

- Once testing types and cost are agreed, the group splits the total cost proportionally.
- Example: a $1,300 total divided evenly among 13 participants is ~$100 each.
- Often only a subset (e.g., 4–5 people) sends in vials for physical analysis; the rest pay in to support the group test  .

**🧪 Step 4: Submitting Samples & Lab Workflow**

- Volunteers ship sealed vials (from the same batch) to the third-party lab.
- Labs like Janoshik or Peptide Test use validated methods (e.g. USP, NF standards) and issue Certificates of Analysis (COAs)  .
- The number of submitted vials (e.g., 3–5) is decided by the group vote and often reflects redundancy or batch variation.

**📄 Step 5: Results & Reporting**

- Labs deliver a COA detailing:
  - Peptide identity confirmation
  - Purity percentage
  - Endotoxin levels
  - Sterility or contaminants
- Reports are shared with all members:
  - Publicly in chat
  - Via spreadsheets
  - In group COA databases

**💬 Why It’s Valuable**

- Enables shared financial burden: group pays less individually.
- Supports transparent decision-making: voting clarifies which tests are most important.
- Builds community trust: verified lab data strengthens vendor credibility.
- Helps groups identify potential issues early (e.g. low purity or high contaminants).
