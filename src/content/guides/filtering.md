A  practical study tested syringe filters (4mm vs 13mm) on reconstituted peptides, measuring volume loss and concentration via HPLC. Key findings: 4mm filters lose ~5-7% volume (0.05-0.07mL from 1mL), while 13mm lose ~28-29% (0.28-0.29mL)—use smaller filters for minimal waste. Peptide concentration changes were negligible (< practical impact), with some samples showing slight increases post-filtration (filters retain water, concentrating peptides). **Bottom line: Filter for sterility, but choose 4mm PES 0.22µm to preserve yield.**

## Study Details

- **Filter Comparison**: 4mm = low volume loss (ideal for 1mL vials); 13mm = higher "dead volume" retention.​
- **Concentration Impact**: Minimal peptide adsorption; filtration primarily affects volume, not purity/potency.​
- **Practical Tip**: Air purge post-filtration recovers extra ~0.01mL from 4mm filters.

 
For 22 micron filters, the Volumetric loss is:
4mm- Loss of .07ml
13mm- Loss of .28ml
33mm- Loss of .65ml
