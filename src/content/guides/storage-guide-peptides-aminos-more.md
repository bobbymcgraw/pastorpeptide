Not sure where to keep your **peptides**? Here’s a simple breakdown:

**Lyophilized (powder)**

![Lyophilized Powder Picture.jpg](https://assets.skool.com/f/04058a020f70494facb3b173e1d1c883/6a83958683514e9da9900d0acb36222a758183465fa7472789e2df69136e7968 "Lyophilized Powder Picture.jpg")

- **Cupboard**: 30-60 days
- **Fridge (39°F)**: 2-3 years
- **Freezer (0°F)**: 10-20 years
- **Deep freezer (-40°)**: 20-30 years
- **Ultra-low freezer (-80°)**: Indefinite

**Reconstituted (mixed with BAC water, or other reconstituting agent)**

![Reconstituted Peptide Image.jpg](https://assets.skool.com/f/04058a020f70494facb3b173e1d1c883/c23db2c3853b4e5ca645f7834bda3e3a77657d831e624ca29e6acafc9ca085e9 "Reconstituted Peptide Image.jpg")

- **Storage:** Must be kept in the **refrigerator** at all times after reconstitution (typically 36–46°F / 2–8°C).
  - **⛔Do not freeze** after mixing.
- **Shelf Life**
  - Most peptides are considered **stable for up to 4-6 weeks** after reconstitution when stored properly.
  - This can vary based on the **length and complexity** of the peptide sequence.🧠**Important Clarification**
- If your peptide is stored beyond 4-6 weeks, it doesn’t become *instantly ineffective*.
  Rather, **gradual degradation** begins to occur — meaning the **potency may decrease over time**, but there is still active peptide present.
  - The rate of degradation depends on the specific compound, storage conditions, and how often it's accessed.
  - Use **visual cues** (cloudiness, color change, floating particles) to help assess usability.

    ![Peptide Dissolved Gelled.jpg](https://assets.skool.com/f/04058a020f70494facb3b173e1d1c883/9b35dd0abfa04b8b94412328e0bece9523a8882ab03c4228b9e9de4187a8012f "Peptide Dissolved Gelled.jpg")
  - Always label your vial with the **date of reconstitution**.

- **Bacteriostatic Water**

  ![Hospira BAC water.jpg](https://assets.skool.com/f/04058a020f70494facb3b173e1d1c883/5d67277027464e32ac20c4355b19fa666ed912f88bc24835b09350594a7bbffd "Hospira BAC water.jpg")

  - Store in a cool, dark place **(**e.g., a cupboard or drawer at room temperature) or the refrigerator.
  - Unopened: Refer to Expiration Date printed on the vial.
    - Note: Multiple lab and user tests have shown that unopened BAC water often remains viable beyond its expiration date. However, **use your own discretion** when deciding whether to use expired BAC water.
  - Opened (pierced): Official guidance recommends discarding 28 days after first use.
    - Important Context: This guideline is based on **clinical settings**, where vials may be pierced several times a day under less controlled conditions.
      - Many peptide users report continuing to use BAC water well beyond 28 days without issue, especially if it’s stored properly and accessed with sterile needles.
      - Again, **use your best judgment**, and discard the vial if it appears cloudy, contaminated, or compromised.

**Keep it simple:**

**Powder = Freezer ❄️**

**Mixed = Fridge**

**BAC water = cupboard or fridge**

# **🧪 Storage Guidelines: Aminos & Amino Blends**

![LCarn Photo.jfif](https://assets.skool.com/f/04058a020f70494facb3b173e1d1c883/2ac2b8ba45e34d34ab60f4f07f0945452da257137076458db9fd8d34446bfa2c "LCarn Photo.jfif")

Amino acid blends (like **L-Carnitine**, **Lipo-C**, or **Super Shred-type injections**) are typically **pre-mixed liquid solutions** that will come in an amber colored vial. They are easier to use than peptides but still require proper storage to ensure safety and potency.
**Before Opening (Unpierced Vial):**

- **Room temperature** is usually acceptable unless otherwise stated on the label.
- Store in a **cool, dark place** away from direct sunlight or heat (e.g., a cupboard or drawer).
- Always check the **expiration date** and avoid freezing the vial.

💡 Some amino blends may include **vitamins (like B12 or B6)** or **lipotropic agents** that are sensitive to heat — cooler storage is better when in doubt.
**After Opening (Pierced Vial):**

- Store in the **refrigerator** (36–46°F / 2–8°C) once the vial has been punctured. This is to help prevent bacterial growth.
- **Use within 30–60 days** depending on the product and frequency of access.
- Label the vial with the **date of first use**.

🔍 **Visual Check:** If the solution becomes cloudy, discolored, or develops particles — discard it.
**Key Tips:**

- Do **not freeze** aminos unless explicitly instructed.
- Avoid leaving the vial at room temperature for long periods after opening.
- If injecting IM or SubQ, always rotate sites to avoid irritation.
