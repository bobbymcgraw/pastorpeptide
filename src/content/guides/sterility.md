Sterility

Sterility testing confirms that a peptide preparation — typically a lyophilized vial — is **free from viable microorganisms** like bacteria, yeast, and mold.
Even in “research use only” settings, this test is an important quality benchmark.

---

## **1. Why Sterility Testing Matters**

- Verifies that **no living contaminants** are present.
- Confirms proper aseptic handling, lyophilization, and packaging.
- Adds **trust and credibility** in light gray market or group-buy settings.
- Essential for anything intended for parenteral (injectable) use.

---

## **2. Testing Methods**

### **A. USP <71> Membrane Filtration** *(gold standard for clear solutions)*

- Reconstitute sample and pass it through a sterile **0.45 µm filter**.
- Filter is placed in:
  - **Fluid Thioglycollate Medium (FTM)** – detects anaerobic bacteria.
  - **Soybean-Casein Digest Medium (SCDM)** – detects aerobic bacteria and fungi.
- Incubate for **14 days** at two different temperatures.
- No growth → Pass.

---

### **B. Direct Inoculation**

- A reconstituted sample is added directly into both media.
- Incubate for **14 days** under aerobic and anaerobic conditions.
- Observe for turbidity or visible microbial growth.

---

## **3. Optional Add-On Tests**

- **Endotoxin Test (LAL Assay)** – Detects bacterial endotoxins.
- **Particulate Matter** – Checks for visible and microscopic particulates.
- **pH & Osmolarity** – Confirms safe reconstitution parameters.

---

## **4. Sample Requirements**

- Usually **1 vials per batch**.
- Reconstitute in sterile bacteriostatic or sterile water before submission.
- Handle in a cleanroom or biosafety cabinet to avoid false positives.

---

## **5. Timeline & Cost**

- Incubation: **14 days** (per USP standards).
- Total turnaround: ~15–18 days.
- Cost: **$150–$350** for sterility
