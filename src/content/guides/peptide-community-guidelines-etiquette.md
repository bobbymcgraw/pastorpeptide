## ✅ Core Rules

- **Stay on topic**
  This community is dedicated to peptides and related research. Discussions about anabolic steroids, SARMs, or controlled substances are not permitted. Post in the correct classroom or category—off-topic posts may be removed or redirected.
- **No medical advice**
  This is an educational space. Do not offer or solicit medical advice. Always consult a licensed professional for health decisions.
- **Search before posting**
  Many questions have already been answered. Use the search bar to explore before starting a new thread.
- **Use clear titles**
  Help others find your post by using descriptive titles (e.g., “Dosage timing for BPC-157” instead of “Help!”).
- **Share sources responsibly**
  If citing studies or articles, use reputable sources. Avoid promoting unverified claims.
- **Do not ask for vendor recommendations**
  We provide a vetted list of reputable vendors to support your research. Each has strengths and limitations—no vendor is universally “best.” All purchases carry inherent risk. Do your own due diligence.
- **No external links to vendors**
  You may discuss vendors, but direct links are not allowed.
- **No spam or self-promotion**
  This includes linking to outside vendors, products, or unrelated content.
- **Report issues**
  If you see a post that violates these rules, contact an admin directly. We’re here to help.

## 🧠 Tone & Culture

- **Be kind, always**
  Assume good intent. Respect different perspectives and avoid sarcasm or snark.
- **Be respectful**
  No bullying, belittling, or personal attacks. Foster a culture of learning and curiosity.
- **Constructive over critical**
  Debate is welcome, but keep it evidence-based and solution-oriented. If you disagree, do so respectfully and without shaming.
- **No swearing or offensive language**
  Keep it clean and professional. This is a learning space—not a locker room.

## 🔒 Safety & Boundaries

- **A vendor will never contact you first**
  If someone reaches out unsolicited, report it immediately.
- **Contact admins directly for concerns**
  If something feels off, don’t hesitate to reach out. We take safety seriously.
