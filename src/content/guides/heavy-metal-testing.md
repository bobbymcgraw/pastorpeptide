Heavy Metals

## **1. What Heavy Metal Testing Is**

Heavy metal testing measures **toxic elemental contamination** in a peptide batch.
Common metals tested include:

- **Lead (Pb)**
- **Arsenic (As)**
- **Cadmium (Cd)**
- **Mercury (Hg)**
  Some labs also screen for **nickel, chromium, cobalt, aluminum, and copper** if relevant.

---

## **2. Why It Matters for Peptides**

- Heavy metals can be introduced from:
  - Contaminated **raw materials**.
  - Poor-quality **glassware or reaction vessels**.
  - Impure **solvents and reagents**.
- In regulated pharma, metal levels are strictly limited under **ICH Q3D guidelines**.
- Even in “research use only” markets, high heavy metal content can indicate **unsafe manufacturing practices**.

---

## **3. Common Testing Methods**

### **A. ICP-MS (Inductively Coupled Plasma Mass Spectrometry)** – *Most Common*

- The gold standard for ultra-trace detection (parts per billion / ppb).
- Works by:
  1. Dissolving peptide sample.
  2. Atomizing it in a plasma torch (~10,000 °C).
  3. Measuring each element’s unique mass-to-charge ratio in the MS detector.

**Pros:** Extremely sensitive, covers a wide range of metals.
**Cons:** Requires specialized lab equipment.

---

### **B. ICP-OES (Inductively Coupled Plasma Optical Emission Spectroscopy)**

- Similar to ICP-MS, but detects light emitted from excited atoms instead of mass.
- Slightly less sensitive than ICP-MS but still highly reliable.

---

### **C. AAS (Atomic Absorption Spectroscopy)**

- Older method using light absorption by vaporized atoms.
- Cheaper, but usually tests only one metal at a time.

---

## **4. Interpreting Results**

Results are typically given in:

- **µg/g (ppm)** or **ng/g (ppb)**.
- Compared to limits set by:
  - **ICH Q3D (Pharmaceutical standards)**.
  - **USP <232>** Elemental Impurities guidelines.

**Example limits for injectables (ICH Q3D, parenteral):**

- Lead: ≤0.5 µg/g
- Arsenic: ≤1.5 µg/g
- Cadmium: ≤0.2 µg/g
- Mercury: ≤0.3 µg/g

A high-quality peptide should be **well below** these limits.

---

## **5. How It Fits Into Peptide QC**

For a complete peptide safety profile:

1. **LC-MS** → Identity & purity.
2. **Sterility & endotoxin** → Microbial safety.
3. **Heavy metal testing** → Inorganic contamination check.
